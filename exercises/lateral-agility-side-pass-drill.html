Speed / Barrel Racing Intermediate 15 min

Lateral Agility: Side-Pass Drill

Focus: Agility, Lateral Work, Responsiveness

Equipment: 4 cones or markers

Arena Diagram

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Set up 4 cones in a line, 10 feet apart. Position your horse parallel to the cone line.
  2. Side-pass from cone 1 to cone 2. The horse should step sideways without moving forward.
  3. Halt at cone 2 for 3 seconds. Then side-pass to cone 3.
  4. Continue to cone 4. Then reverse and side-pass back to cone 1.
  5. The side-pass develops the lateral agility needed for tight barrel turns.
  6. Practice in both directions for equal development.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • The horse walking forward instead of moving purely sideways.
  • Only the front end or hind end moving laterally (not the whole body together).
  • The horse refusing to side-pass because it hasn't been taught the cue.
  • Moving too quickly and losing the quality of the lateral steps.

Variations & Progressions

  • Side-pass over a pole for added precision.
  • Decrease cone spacing to 8 feet for tighter lateral work.
  • Side-pass at the jog for a more athletic exercise.
Tip: Lateral agility translates directly to barrel turns. A horse that side-passes easily will turn barrels smoothly.
