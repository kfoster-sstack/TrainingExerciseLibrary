Flatwork / Dressage Beginner 15 min

Canter Circle with Walk Transitions

Focus: Transitions, Balance, Engagement

Equipment: 4 cones or markers

Arena Diagram

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Set up a 20-meter circle. Begin at the working walk on the correct lead.
  2. At the first quarter-point cone, transition to walk for 4-6 strides.
  3. At the next quarter-point, pick up the walk again. The transition should be prompt and on the correct lead.
  4. Continue this pattern: walk three-quarters, walk one-quarter, around the circle.
  5. After 3 repetitions, reverse the ratio: walk three-quarters and walk only one-quarter of the circle.
  6. The walk-to-walk transitions on a circle test your horse's responsiveness and balance.
  7. Finish with a full circle of balanced walk, then walk and stretch.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Picking up the wrong lead after the trot interlude.
  • The trot section becoming rushed as the horse anticipates the canter depart.
  • Losing the circle shape during transitions.
  • The canter becoming flat and running after multiple transitions.

Variations & Progressions

  • Use walk transitions instead of trot for a more advanced collection exercise.
  • Reduce to 2-3 trot strides between canter segments for sharper transitions.
  • Perform on a 15-meter circle to increase the balance demand.
Tip: If the horse picks up the wrong lead, calmly trot and try again. Don't punish, just redirect.
