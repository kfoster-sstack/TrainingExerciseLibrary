Jumping Intermediate 25 min

Collected Trot Pole Serpentine

Focus: Accuracy, Bend, Balance

Equipment: 6 ground poles

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Set up 3 pairs of collected trot poles scattered across the arena, each pair spaced 4.5 feet apart.
  2. Design a serpentine path that connects all three pairs with smooth curves.
  3. Collected Trot the serpentine, passing through each pair of poles on the bending line.
  4. Maintain the collected trot rhythm through each pair and through each turn.
  5. The turns between pole pairs develop the horse's ability to change bend while maintaining focus.
  6. Repeat the serpentine 3-4 times, then reverse the direction for practice on the other rein.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Cutting the turns too tight between pole pairs.
  • The horse speeding up between poles in anticipation.
  • Losing the trot rhythm over the poles because the approach angle isn't straight enough.
  • Not planning the turns far enough in advance.

Variations & Progressions

  • Raise the poles to cavaletti height for increased difficulty.
  • Add small cross-rails instead of ground poles.
  • Canter the serpentine with simple lead changes between pole pairs.
Tip: Think of this as a mini-course without the jumps. The skills you develop here — turn planning, rhythm, line accuracy — directly transfer to course riding.
