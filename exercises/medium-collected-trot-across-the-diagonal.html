Flatwork / Dressage Intermediate 25 min

Medium Collected Trot Across the Diagonal

Focus: Transitions, Impulsion, Adjustability

Equipment: Letter markers

Arena Diagram

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Establish a balanced working collected trot. Prepare for the diagonal line.
  2. At the corner letter, turn across the diagonal. In the first 3-4 strides, ask the horse to lengthen into medium collected trot.
  3. Allow the stride to grow longer and more ground-covering. The tempo stays the same — only the stride length increases.
  4. Your posting should become bigger and more forward to match the longer stride.
  5. Maintain the medium collected trot across the diagonal until 3-4 strides before the track.
  6. Collect back to working collected trot before reaching the track. The collection should be smooth, not abrupt.
  7. Change direction and repeat on the other diagonal.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • The horse running faster instead of lengthening the stride.
  • Losing balance and falling onto the forehand during the medium trot.
  • Not collecting soon enough and arriving at the track unbalanced.
  • The rider gripping with the knees and blocking the horse's shoulder movement.

Variations & Progressions

  • Perform medium trot on the long side for a different spatial challenge.
  • Add a 10-meter circle at X in the working trot before continuing medium trot.
  • Progress to extended trot across the diagonal for even greater lengthening.
Tip: Medium trot should feel like the horse is pushing more from behind, not pulling from the front. The engine is in the hindquarters.
