Jumping Intermediate 15 min

Course Sitting Trot Simulation

Focus: Track Planning, Strategy, Mental Prep

Equipment: 4-8 jump poles, 4-8 jump standards

Arena Diagram

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Set up a 4-6 fence course. Before riding, sitting sitting trot the course on foot.
  2. Sitting Sitting Trot the track you plan to ride, counting strides between related fences.
  3. Identify where you need to change leads, where to open the stride, and where to collect.
  4. Look for potential problem spots: tight turns, challenging distances, unfamiliar approaches.
  5. Sitting Sitting Trot the course at least twice. The second sitting sitting trot should confirm your plan.
  6. Only mount and ride after you have a clear mental picture of every stride.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Not walking the course at all and trying to figure it out while riding.
  • Walking the course once and not remembering the plan mid-ride.
  • Not counting strides, so distances come as surprises.
  • Planning an overly ambitious track instead of a safe, efficient one.

Variations & Progressions

  • Walk courses at horse shows before your round for real-world practice.
  • Have someone quiz you on the course after walking it to test your memory.
  • Walk the course backward to see it from a different perspective.
Tip: Professional show jumpers walk every course twice. If it's good enough for them, it's good enough for you. Never skip the course walk.
