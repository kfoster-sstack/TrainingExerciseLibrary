Liberty Circle Work

Focus: Communication, Bond, Control

Equipment: Round pen or small arena

Arena Diagram

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. With the horse at liberty in a round pen or small arena, position yourself at the center.
  2. Use body language to send the horse onto a circle around you: step toward its haunches, raise your energy.
  3. Once moving on the circle, use your body position to control the pace: move toward the haunches for faster, move toward the shoulder to slow.
  4. Ask for a change of direction by stepping ahead of the horse's driveline.
  5. Practice walk-trot transitions using voice and body language only.
  6. After working, invite the horse to come to the center and reward the connection.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Escalating energy too quickly, causing the horse to run in panic instead of responding calmly.
  • Poor body positioning — facing the horse's eye line drives it away; facing the shoulder invites it.
  • Not reading the horse's body language: licking/chewing = processing, inside ear = attention on you.
  • Working too long at liberty, causing the horse to tune out.

Variations & Progressions

  • Add halts at liberty on the circle.
  • Work toward calling the horse to you from across the pen.
  • Introduce simple patterns at liberty: circle, change direction, halt, back up.
Tip: Liberty work is about conversation, not control. The horse chooses to listen because it trusts and respects you.
