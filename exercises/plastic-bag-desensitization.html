Plastic Bag Desensitization

Focus: Confidence, Desensitization, Patience

Equipment: 2-3 plastic bags, Halter and leadrope

Arena Diagram

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Crinkle a plastic bag in your hand while standing 20 feet from your horse. Let it hear the sound from a distance.
  2. Slowly approach the horse while gently crinkling. If the horse holds still, reward and retreat.
  3. When the horse accepts you nearby with the bag, touch its shoulder with the bag and immediately remove it. Praise.
  4. Gradually increase the duration of the bag touching the horse's body.
  5. Progress to rubbing the bag along the neck, back, and sides.
  6. The final stage: the horse stands calmly while you crinkle, wave, and rub the bag all over its body.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Approaching too fast with a crinkling bag, triggering a flight response.
  • Not giving the horse enough time to process at each stage.
  • Punishing the horse for moving away — it's natural for a prey animal to fear crinkly objects.
  • Skipping stages because the horse seemed okay initially.

Variations & Progressions

  • Tie bags to the fence and let the horse investigate on its own.
  • Ride past crinkly bags for mounted desensitization.
  • Use bags in different colors and sizes for variety.
Tip: Approach and retreat is the key principle: approach with the scary object, retreat the moment the horse shows any calm acceptance.
