Flatwork / Dressage Beginner 15 min

Counting Strides Between Letters at the Trot

Focus: Rhythm, Awareness, Adjustability

Equipment: Letter markers

Arena Diagram

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. At the working trot, count the number of strides from one letter to the next along the long side.
  2. Ride the long side and count: H to E might be 12 strides, for example.
  3. Now ride the same long side and try to fit exactly 12 strides again. Same rhythm, same stride length.
  4. Change it: try to fit 14 strides (shorter, more collected) or 10 strides (longer, more extended).
  5. Ride back and forth, changing the stride count target each time.
  6. This exercise teaches you to feel and control stride length, which is essential for jumping and dressage.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Losing count because the rider stops focusing mid-way.
  • Changing the tempo instead of the stride length to hit the count.
  • Only being able to lengthen but not shorten (or vice versa).
  • Counting every stride of the posting trot instead of every other one (posting trot has two phases per stride).

Variations & Progressions

  • Count strides on a diagonal and adjust: lengthen or shorten to hit a target number.
  • Apply the counting exercise at the canter for jumping preparation.
  • Count strides in a combination: letter A to B = 12, letter B to C = 14 (different stride lengths).
Tip: This skill directly transfers to jumping distances. If you can adjust flatwork strides on demand, you can ride any distance to a fence.
