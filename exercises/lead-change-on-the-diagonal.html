Lead Change on the Diagonal

Focus: Lead Changes, Balance, Timing

Equipment: 4 cones or markers

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Lope on the right lead along the long side of the arena.
  2. Turn across the diagonal, maintaining the right lead.
  3. At the center of the diagonal, ask for a flying lead change to the left lead.
  4. The change should happen in one stride: both front and hind legs switch simultaneously.
  5. Continue loping on the left lead to the new track.
  6. If the horse doesn't change cleanly, come back to the jog and try again.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Asking for the change too early or too late on the diagonal.
  • The horse changing only in front (late behind), creating a disunited canter.
  • Using dramatic body aids that throw the horse off balance.
  • Practicing too many changes in one session, causing the horse to become tense.

Variations & Progressions

  • Practice on a figure-eight for repeated changes in both directions.
  • Work toward automatic changes where the horse changes at the center without strong aids.
  • Use diagonal changes as preparation for lead changes within reining patterns.
Tip: A clean flying change looks effortless. If it looks dramatic, the preparation was insufficient. Go back to simple changes.
