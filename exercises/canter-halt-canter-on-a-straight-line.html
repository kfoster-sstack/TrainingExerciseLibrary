Canter-Halt-Canter on a Straight Line

Focus: Collection, Obedience, Responsiveness

Equipment: 4 cones or markers

Arena Diagram

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Set up 4 cones in a line, 60 feet apart, along the long side.
  2. Canter from cone 1 to cone 2. At cone 2, halt. Stand for 3 seconds.
  3. From the halt, immediately pick up canter to cone 3. Halt at cone 3.
  4. Continue the canter-halt-canter pattern through all 4 cones.
  5. The halts should be prompt and square. The canter departures should be clean and on the correct lead.
  6. This exercise develops the responsiveness needed for adjustability in jumping courses.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Trotting between the halt and canter instead of making clean transitions.
  • The halts being messy — the horse walking forward or swinging sideways.
  • Losing the correct lead on the canter departures.
  • The canter becoming tense and rushed from the repeated halts.

Variations & Progressions

  • Reduce to 3 cones for a simpler version.
  • Replace halts with walk transitions (3 walk steps) for an easier version.
  • Add this exercise before a gymnastics session to sharpen the horse's responsiveness.
Tip: If your horse can halt cleanly from canter and depart cleanly from halt, it has the obedience to handle any distance question in a course.
