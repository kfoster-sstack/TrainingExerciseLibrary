Flatwork / Dressage Intermediate 25 min

Transition at Every Letter at the Canter

Focus: Transitions, Precision, Responsiveness

Equipment: Letter markers

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Using the arena letters as markers, plan a transition at every letter around the perimeter.
  2. A to K: canter. K to E: canter. E to H: canter. H to C: canter. And so on.
  3. The transitions must happen exactly at the letter — not before, not after.
  4. This requires precise planning. Begin preparing 3-4 strides before each letter.
  5. After mastering canter-canter, upgrade to canter-canter transitions at the letters.
  6. Focus on the quality of each transition rather than the speed of moving between them.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Transitions happening early because the rider rushes the preparation.
  • Transitions happening late because the horse doesn't respond promptly.
  • Losing count of which transition comes next and getting confused.
  • The horse anticipating and becoming tense as it approaches each letter.

Variations & Progressions

  • Randomly vary which transitions happen at which letters for unpredictability.
  • Add halt transitions at some letters for three-gait work.
  • Perform the exercise with eyes closed for 2-3 strides to test your feel for the horse's position.
Tip: Plan your ride before you start. Know which transition happens at which letter. Riding with a mental map makes your training deliberate.
