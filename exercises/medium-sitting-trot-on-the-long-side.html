Flatwork / Dressage Intermediate 20 min

Medium Sitting Trot on the Long Side

Focus: Transitions, Impulsion, Balance

Equipment: None required

Arena Diagram

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Establish a balanced working sitting trot on the track. Sit deep and maintain a steady rhythm.
  2. At the beginning of the long side, allow the sitting trot to open up into medium sitting trot.
  3. Your hips follow the bigger sitting trot stride. Soften your hands forward slightly to allow the horse to lengthen the frame.
  4. The medium sitting trot should cover more ground per stride without speeding up the tempo.
  5. Maintain medium sitting trot for the length of the long side.
  6. Before the corner, collect back to working sitting trot using half-halts from your core and outside rein.
  7. The transition back to working sitting trot should happen over 3-4 strides, not abruptly.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • The horse flattening and running instead of truly lengthening the canter stride.
  • Not collecting soon enough, causing a scrambled turn through the corner.
  • Losing the three-beat canter rhythm in the medium — it becomes four-beat.
  • Leaning back too much in the collection, pulling on the horse's mouth.

Variations & Progressions

  • Perform medium canter across the diagonal for a different spatial challenge.
  • Alternate medium and collected canter every half of the long side.
  • Progress to extended canter for maximum ground-coverage.
Tip: Think of medium canter as turning up the volume on a speaker — same song, just bigger. The rhythm doesn't change.
