Jumping Intermediate 25 min

Bounce Grid: Two Cross-Rails at the Sitting Trot

Focus: Technique, Balance, Confidence

Equipment: 4 jump poles, 4 jump standards

Arena Diagram

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Set up 2 small cross-rails 9-10 feet apart (bounce distance).
  2. Approach at the sitting trot. The horse will jump the first cross-rail, land, and immediately jump the second.
  3. There is no stride between the fences — the horse bounces from one to the next.
  4. Keep your upper body slightly behind vertical and maintain a steady leg contact.
  5. Allow your arms to follow the horse's mouth over each jump. Don't drop the contact.
  6. Repeat 4-6 times. Start with the cross-rails very low (12 inches) and gradually raise them.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Approaching too fast, causing the horse to jump too big and land too far into the bounce.
  • The rider getting left behind on the first jump and interfering with the second.
  • Not enough impulsion, causing the horse to stop between the two fences.
  • Leaning too far forward, putting weight on the horse's forehand.

Variations & Progressions

  • Add a third cross-rail for a three-element bounce.
  • Raise the fences to verticals at 2 feet for more effort.
  • Place a ground pole one stride (18 feet) before the bounce as a placing pole.
Tip: Bounces are the best gymnastic exercise. They teach the horse to use its body correctly without relying on speed.
