Two-Stride Related Distance at the Sitting Trot

Focus: Stride Control, Adjustability, Accuracy

Equipment: 4 jump poles, 4 jump standards

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Set up 2 fences 36 feet apart (two normal sitting trot strides).
  2. Approach at the working sitting trot. After the first fence, the horse should land and take exactly 2 strides to the second.
  3. Keep your leg on between the fences. Look up at the second fence, not down.
  4. If the horse adds a third stride, approach with more impulsion next time.
  5. If the horse gets to the second fence in one big stride, collect on the approach.
  6. Practice until 2 strides feels automatic.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Panicking between the fences and pulling the horse back.
  • Not enough impulsion, causing a chip-in third stride.
  • The horse jumping flat over the first fence, landing too far into the line.
  • The rider interfering with the horse's natural stride between fences.

Variations & Progressions

  • Try 35 feet for a steady 2 strides and 37 feet for a forward 2 strides.
  • Add a third fence at 36 feet for a 2-stride to 2-stride combination.
  • Ride the 2-stride in 3 strides (collected) for advanced adjustability.
Tip: Two strides is the most common combination distance. If you can ride it cleanly every time, you can handle anything the course designer throws at you.
