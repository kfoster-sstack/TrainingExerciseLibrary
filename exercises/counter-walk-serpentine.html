Flatwork / Dressage Beginner 20 min

Counter-Walk Serpentine

Focus: Balance, Collection, Straightness

Equipment: Letter markers

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Establish a balanced right-lead walk on the track.
  2. Ride a shallow serpentine along the long side, maintaining the right lead throughout.
  3. The loop should take you 5 meters off the track and back. You will be in counter-walk on the inward portion.
  4. Maintain a slight right flexion even when curving left. The horse must hold the lead from your aids, not the direction.
  5. Ride 3-4 shallow loops along the long side, then straighten and walk.
  6. Change leads and repeat the counter-walk serpentine on the left lead.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • The horse swapping leads during the counter-canter portions.
  • The loops becoming too deep, making the counter-canter unstable.
  • Losing the three-beat rhythm in the counter-canter sections.
  • The rider gripping and becoming tense, transferring anxiety to the horse.

Variations & Progressions

  • Deepen the loops to 10 meters off the track for more demanding counter-canter.
  • Add flying changes at the peak of each loop for upper-level work.
  • Ride the serpentine in true canter, then immediately repeat in counter-canter for comparison.
Tip: Counter-canter serpentines build the straightness and balance needed for flying changes. The horse learns to hold the lead until you say otherwise.
