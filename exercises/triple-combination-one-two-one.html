Jumping Intermediate 30 min

Triple Combination: One-Two-One

Focus: Technique, Adjustability, Scope

Equipment: 6 jump poles, 6 jump standards

Arena Diagram

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Set up 3 fences: first to second is one stride (18 feet), second to third is two strides (36 feet).
  2. Approach at the trot. The horse will jump, take one stride, jump, take two strides, and jump the final fence.
  3. The changing stride counts test the horse's adjustability within a combination.
  4. Keep your eyes up and your leg active. Let the distances teach the horse.
  5. Start with all fences as small cross-rails. Raise gradually as the horse gains confidence.
  6. Repeat 4-6 times. The horse should smooth out with each pass.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Approaching in canter (should be trot for consistent first distance).
  • Interfering with the horse's natural stride between fences.
  • Getting ahead of the motion on any of the three jumping efforts.
  • Setting the fences too high before the horse masters the distances.

Variations & Progressions

  • Reverse the distances: two strides first, then one stride.
  • Raise the middle fence highest for a scope exercise.
  • Change to bounced to one-stride (10 feet, then 18 feet) for a technical variation.
Tip: Triple combinations appear in every show jumping course. Master them in training and they'll be your strength in competition.
