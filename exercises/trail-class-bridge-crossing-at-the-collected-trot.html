Western Intermediate 20 min

Trail Class: Bridge Crossing at the Collected Trot

Focus: Confidence, Body Awareness, Trust

Equipment: Bridge or platform (or plywood sheet)

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Position the bridge or platform in the arena. Lead the horse to it first, allowing it to investigate.
  2. Once comfortable, ride the horse toward the bridge at the collected collected trot.
  3. Allow the horse to look at the bridge. When it takes a step toward it, praise.
  4. The horse should collected collected trot onto the bridge with its front feet, then bring the hind feet on.
  5. Stand on the bridge for 3-5 seconds. Then collected collected trot off calmly.
  6. Repeat until the horse collected collected trots over the bridge without hesitation.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Forcing the horse onto the bridge before it's comfortable.
  • Punishing hesitation — the horse is being cautious, which is natural.
  • Rushing over the bridge instead of standing on it.
  • Not letting the horse look at the bridge before asking it to step on.

Variations & Progressions

  • Walk over the bridge from both directions.
  • Stand on the bridge for 30+ seconds for patience development.
  • Jog over the bridge once the walk is confident.
Tip: The bridge is a trust exercise. The horse trusts you enough to step onto a surface that sounds and feels different. Reward that trust.
