Groundwork Beginner 20 min

Lunging Over Ground Poles

Focus: Engagement, Balance, Coordination

Equipment: Lunge line, Lunge whip, 4 ground poles

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Set up 4 ground poles on the lunging circle, evenly spaced about a quarter of the circle apart.
  2. The poles should be fanned: narrow end toward the center (3 feet apart), wide end toward the outside (5 feet apart).
  3. Send the horse out at the walk over the poles. It should walk calmly over each one.
  4. Progress to the trot. The horse will naturally lift its legs higher over the poles.
  5. Watch for the horse rounding its back and engaging its topline — this is the desired response.
  6. Work 3-4 minutes in each direction. Remove poles and compare the quality of the trot before and after.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Poles placed too close together or too far apart for the horse's natural stride.
  • The horse stepping on or knocking poles because the circle size puts it at an awkward spacing.
  • Not adjusting the circle size to match the horse's stride over the poles.
  • Working too long over poles, causing the horse to become careless.

Variations & Progressions

  • Raise the poles to 4-6 inches on cavaletti blocks for more engagement.
  • Use 6 poles for a longer pole exercise on the circle.
  • Add transitions over the poles: walk over two, trot over two.
Tip: Lunging over poles is one of the best tools for building topline muscle without a rider's weight. Use it liberally for young or rehabbing horses.
