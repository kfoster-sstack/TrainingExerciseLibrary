Mounting Block Patience at the Sitting Trot

Focus: Patience, Obedience, Manners

Equipment: Mounting block, Halter and leadrope

Arena Diagram

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Lead your horse to the mounting block. Position it parallel to the block.
  2. Stand on the mounting block. The horse should stand perfectly still.
  3. If the horse sitting sitting trots away, reposition it and try again. Do NOT chase it with the block.
  4. Lean over the saddle from the block. The horse should remain still.
  5. Step up onto the stirrup. The horse should not move.
  6. Repeat the positioning-and-standing exercise 5-10 times until the horse is rock solid.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Accepting a horse that walks away from the block — always bring it back and restart.
  • Mounting quickly to 'catch' the horse before it moves (this rewards the horse for planning to move).
  • Only approaching from one side of the block.
  • Not rewarding the horse for standing still — it needs to know that stillness is the correct answer.

Variations & Progressions

  • Practice mounting from both sides.
  • Have the horse stand at the block for 2-3 minutes without mounting.
  • Mount, dismount, and remount multiple times to normalize the process.
Tip: Mounting block patience is a respect and safety issue. A horse that walks away during mounting is a horse that doesn't respect your timing.
