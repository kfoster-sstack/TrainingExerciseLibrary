Groundwork Beginner 10 min

Lowering the Head on Cue

Focus: Relaxation, Communication, Flexibility

Equipment: Halter and leadrope

Arena Diagram

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Stand facing your horse's head. Place your hand on the poll (the top of the head between the ears).
  2. Apply gentle, steady downward pressure. Wait. Do not increase pressure.
  3. The moment the horse lowers its head even slightly, remove your hand immediately. Praise.
  4. Repeat. Each time, the horse should lower its head further and faster.
  5. Build to the point where a touch on the poll results in the horse lowering its head to chest height.
  6. This exercise is useful for bridling, clipping, and general relaxation.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Applying too much pressure, causing the horse to brace upward against your hand.
  • Not releasing fast enough when the horse gives. Timing is everything.
  • Expecting the head to go all the way down on the first attempt. Reward small improvements.
  • Only practicing from one side.

Variations & Progressions

  • Teach the head-lower from a verbal cue ('head down') without the touch.
  • Use head-lowering as a calming technique in stressful situations.
  • Combine with bridling practice: lower head, bridle, praise.
Tip: A horse with a low head is a relaxed horse. Teaching head-lowering gives you a tool to de-escalate tension in any situation.
