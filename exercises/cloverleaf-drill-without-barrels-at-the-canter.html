Cloverleaf Drill Without Barrels at the Canter

Focus: Pattern Accuracy, Fitness, Agility

Equipment: 4 cones or markers

Arena Diagram

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Set up cones in a triangle (barrel pattern positions). Add a fourth cone at the start point.
  2. Canter the entire pattern as a cloverleaf: start, circle cone 1, cross to cone 2, circle, cross to cone 3, circle, run home.
  3. Keep the canter steady and rhythmic. Don't speed up or slow down.
  4. The cloverleaf shape should be consistent: equal-sized circles at each cone.
  5. Practice 4-5 complete patterns. Focus on smooth transitions between straight lines and circles.
  6. This is a warm-up exercise that keeps the pattern fresh without adding speed or intensity.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Circles at each cone being different sizes.
  • The horse anticipating barrel work and getting excited when it sees the pattern.
  • Not maintaining a consistent jog throughout the exercise.
  • Skipping the straight-line portions between circles.

Variations & Progressions

  • Lope the cloverleaf at a slow, collected pace.
  • Add transitions within the pattern: jog circles, walk straight lines.
  • Reverse the pattern direction for variety.
Tip: The cloverleaf drill at the jog is the best warm-up for barrel horses. It keeps the pattern muscle memory without the physical stress of speed work.
