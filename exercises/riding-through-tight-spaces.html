Riding Through Tight Spaces

Focus: Confidence, Body Awareness, Control

Equipment: 2 barrels or standards, 4 ground poles

Arena Diagram

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Create a narrow passage using barrels or standards, approximately 4 feet wide.
  2. Walk your horse toward the narrow passage. Allow it to assess the space.
  3. Keep your legs on firmly. The horse may try to stop or veer around the narrow gap.
  4. Guide the horse straight through the passage using equal rein and leg aids.
  5. Once through, walk forward 10 strides and halt. Praise the horse.
  6. Repeat 4-6 times. Gradually narrow the passage to 3.5 feet if the horse is comfortable.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Not keeping enough forward impulsion, allowing the horse to stop and refuse.
  • The horse scraping the rider's legs on the standards because the passage is too narrow.
  • Approaching at an angle instead of straight on.
  • Giving up and going around the passage when the horse hesitates.

Variations & Progressions

  • Walk through with your arms out to the side to simulate carrying saddlebags.
  • Trot through the passage for increased confidence challenge.
  • Create a longer corridor (10 feet long) for a sustained narrow-space experience.
Tip: Tight spaces on the trail are inevitable — between trees, rocks, or fences. A horse that's practiced in tight spaces stays calm when it matters.
