Leading at the Trot In-Hand at the Trot

Focus: Fitness, Responsiveness, Energy

Equipment: Halter and leadrope

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Begin by troting your horse at a brisk pace. When you're both moving well, cluck and trot.
  2. Your horse should immediately match your pace and trot beside you at the shoulder position.
  3. Maintain the trot for 20-30 steps, then return to trot with a 'whoa' and slowing your feet.
  4. The transition from trot to trot should be prompt — within 2 steps of your cluck.
  5. The transition from trot to trot should also be prompt — the horse shouldn't trot past you.
  6. Practice 8-10 trot-trot-trot transitions. The horse should become more responsive each time.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Running out of breath because you're not fit enough to jog with the horse.
  • The horse pulling ahead at the trot because it has more energy than you.
  • Not establishing clear difference between your walk and trot cues.
  • Letting the horse break to canter when you only asked for trot.

Variations & Progressions

  • Add halt from the trot for a more advanced transition.
  • Practice trotting in-hand over ground poles for coordination.
  • Lead at the trot in-hand as part of your warm-up routine before every ride.
Tip: In-hand trotting is a great fitness check for you too. If you can't jog with your horse for 2 minutes, consider adding some running to your own fitness plan.
