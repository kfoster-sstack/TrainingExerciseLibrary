Flatwork / Dressage Beginner 15 min

Half-Circle and Return to Track

Focus: Bend, Transitions, Accuracy

Equipment: 2 cones or markers

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Starting from the track, ride a half-circle of 10 meters into the arena, then ride a diagonal line back to the track.
  2. Begin at a letter on the long side (like E or B). Turn off the track and ride the half-circle.
  3. At the point farthest from the track, you should be 10 meters from the rail.
  4. Now ride a straight diagonal line back to the track, arriving several meters ahead of where you started.
  5. The diagonal portion should be ridden straight with the horse slightly positioned to the outside to prepare for the new bend on the track.
  6. Practice this movement in both directions. Pay attention to the transition from curved to straight line.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Making the half-circle too shallow, never truly reaching 10 meters from the track.
  • Losing the bend during the half-circle and cutting corners.
  • Not straightening the horse on the diagonal return line.
  • Arriving back at the track at an awkward angle instead of smoothly merging.

Variations & Progressions

  • Increase to a 15-meter half-circle for a shallower return angle.
  • Perform the exercise at the canter, including a lead change if crossing the center.
  • Add a transition to walk at the farthest point of the half-circle, then trot the return.
Tip: This movement appears in many dressage tests. Practice it until the half-circle and return feel like one flowing movement.
