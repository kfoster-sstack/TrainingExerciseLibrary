Ground Pole Grid: Four-Stride Line at the Walk

Focus: Stride Control, Accuracy, Adjustability

Equipment: 2 ground poles, 4 cones or markers

Arena Diagram

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Set up 2 ground poles with 60 feet between them (4 normal walk strides).
  2. Place cones alongside to mark a straight track.
  3. Approach at the working walk. Count your strides: 1-2-3-4. The horse should arrive at the second pole in exactly 4 strides.
  4. If you get 5 strides, open your horse's stride next time. If you get 3, collect more.
  5. Now try to fit 5 strides in the same distance (shorter, more collected strides).
  6. Then try 3 strides (longer, more open strides).
  7. This adjustability is the foundation of all course riding.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Counting strides wrong — start counting when the horse lands after the first pole.
  • Pulling on the reins to add strides instead of using your seat and half-halts.
  • Kicking to remove strides instead of opening your body and allowing a bigger canter.
  • Not approaching on a straight line, making the distance longer or shorter than intended.

Variations & Progressions

  • Set up two different four-stride lines and ride them on a course.
  • Add cross-rails instead of ground poles for a more realistic exercise.
  • Practice the exercise counting down: 4-3-2-1 to develop awareness of where each stride falls.
Tip: Four strides is the most common related distance in show jumping. Master this and you can ride any course.
