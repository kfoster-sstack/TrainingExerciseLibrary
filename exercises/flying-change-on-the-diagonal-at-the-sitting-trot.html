Flying Change on the Diagonal at the Sitting Trot

Focus: Transitions, Balance, Collection

Equipment: Letter markers

Arena Diagram

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Establish a balanced sitting trot on the left lead. Collect for 3-4 strides to prepare.
  2. Turn across the diagonal. Maintain the left lead until you pass X.
  3. After X, straighten the horse completely for 1-2 strides.
  4. Apply the new outside leg (left leg) behind the girth while shifting your weight slightly to the new inside seat bone.
  5. The horse should change both front and hind legs in a single stride. This is a clean flying change.
  6. If the horse only changes in front (late behind), return to sitting trot and try again later.
  7. Practice 2-3 changes per session maximum. Quality over quantity.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Asking for the change too early before the horse is straight.
  • Late behind — the horse changes the front legs but not the hind legs in the same stride.
  • The horse becoming tense and running after the change.
  • The rider throwing their body or swinging their legs dramatically to ask for the change.

Variations & Progressions

  • Practice changes on the center line at specific letters for precision.
  • Work toward tempi changes (every 4 strides, every 3, every 2, every stride).
  • Ride a figure-eight with flying changes at X.
Tip: A good flying change looks effortless. If it looks dramatic, something in the preparation was missing. Go back to simple changes and try again.
