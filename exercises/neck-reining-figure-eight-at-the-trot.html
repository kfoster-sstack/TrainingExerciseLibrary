Neck Reining Figure-Eight at the Trot

Focus: Steering, Lead Changes, Accuracy

Equipment: 2 cones or markers

Arena Diagram

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Set up 2 cones 30 feet apart. You'll ride a figure-eight around them at the trot.
  2. Trot around the first cone on the correct lead (left lead for circling left).
  3. At the midpoint between cones, perform a simple change to the new lead.
  4. Trot around the second cone on the new lead.
  5. The figure-eight should be two equal circles. The simple change should be smooth and quick.
  6. Practice until the neck rein alone handles the direction changes.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Circles being different sizes — they should be mirror images.
  • Picking up the wrong lead after the simple change.
  • The simple change being too long (too many walk or jog strides).
  • Using direct rein instead of neck rein for the figure-eight turns.

Variations & Progressions

  • Progress to flying lead changes at the center of the figure-eight.
  • Vary the circle size: 20 feet vs. 40 feet for adjustability practice.
  • Add speed changes: slow lope on one circle, faster lope on the other.
Tip: The figure-eight is the foundation of reining patterns. Master it with perfect circles and clean changes before adding speed.
