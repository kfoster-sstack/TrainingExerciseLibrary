Western Intermediate 20 min

Trail Class: Side-Pass Over a Pole

Focus: Lateral Work, Precision, Body Control

Equipment: 1 ground pole

Arena Diagram

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Position your horse perpendicular to a ground pole, with its belly directly over the pole.
  2. Ask for a side-pass to the right: left neck rein, left leg pressure, right leg supports.
  3. The horse should move sideways along the pole without stepping on it or moving forward/backward.
  4. Side-pass 4-6 steps to the right, then reverse and side-pass to the left.
  5. The front and hind feet should cross over simultaneously. Both ends of the horse move together.
  6. This is a standard trail class obstacle and an excellent test of lateral control.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • The horse stepping on the pole instead of staying centered over it.
  • Moving forward or backward instead of purely sideways.
  • Only the front end or hind end moving (not a true side-pass).
  • The horse refusing to move sideways and walking off the pole.

Variations & Progressions

  • Side-pass between two parallel poles for a tighter corridor.
  • Side-pass in an L-shape around a corner.
  • Increase the speed of the side-pass for show efficiency.
Tip: The pole forces accuracy because you can see (and hear) if the horse steps on it. Use the pole as your judge.
