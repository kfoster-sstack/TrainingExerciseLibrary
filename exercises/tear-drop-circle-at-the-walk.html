Flatwork / Dressage Beginner 15 min

Tear-Drop Circle at the Walk

Focus: Accuracy, Bend, Straightness

Equipment: 2 cones or markers

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. A tear-drop shape combines a straight line with a half-circle. Start at A or C, ride straight toward X.
  2. At X, begin a 10-meter half-circle turning either left or right.
  3. Complete the half-circle so you are now facing back the way you came, aligned with the center line.
  4. Ride straight back down the center line to your starting letter.
  5. The key is the transition from straight to curved: your horse should accept the bend smoothly without resistance.
  6. Repeat the tear-drop 3 times in each direction, improving the accuracy of your turns each time.
  7. Progress by performing the tear-drop at the walk, maintaining rhythm through the shape change.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Starting the half-circle too early or too late, missing the X centerpoint.
  • Losing straightness on the center line portions.
  • The horse bulging through the outside shoulder on the curved portion.
  • Rushing through the shape instead of maintaining a steady tempo.

Variations & Progressions

  • Ride the tear-drop at the canter, using a downward transition for the half-circle.
  • Make the half-circle portion smaller (8 meters) for more collection demand.
  • Chain two tear-drops together: one left, one right, creating a continuous flowing pattern.
Tip: The tear-drop is excellent for test preparation since many dressage tests require riding to X and turning.
