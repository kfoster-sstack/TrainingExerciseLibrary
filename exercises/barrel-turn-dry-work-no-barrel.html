Barrel Turn Dry Work: No Barrel

Focus: Turns, Bend, Muscle Memory

Equipment: 4 cones or markers

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Place a single cone where a barrel would be. This is your turn point.
  2. Lope toward the cone on your approach line. 4-5 strides before the cone, rate to a jog.
  3. Jog a tight circle around the cone (representing the barrel turn).
  4. After the circle, lope out on your departure line.
  5. Practice 6-8 turns around the cone at this slow speed.
  6. The slow-speed practice builds muscle memory for the turn shape without stressing the horse.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Turning too tight and losing impulsion.
  • Not rating enough before the turn, arriving at the cone too fast.
  • Only practicing turns in one direction.
  • Skipping the slow work and always training at speed.

Variations & Progressions

  • Gradually increase the speed of the approach and turn.
  • Practice with a barrel instead of a cone for more realistic spacing.
  • Chain 3 turns together (representing all 3 barrels) for pattern flow.
Tip: Slow practice builds perfect form. Fast practice reveals weaknesses. Do both, but start with slow.
