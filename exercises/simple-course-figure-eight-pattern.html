Jumping Intermediate 30 min

Simple Course: Figure-Eight Pattern

Focus: Track Planning, Adjustability, Confidence

Equipment: 4 jump poles, 4 jump standards, 4 cones or markers

Arena Diagram

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Set up 2 small fences, one on each 20-meter circle of a figure-eight pattern.
  2. Start by cantering a circle over one fence, then change direction through the center to jump the second fence.
  3. After each fence, establish a balanced canter on the circle before approaching the next.
  4. The change of direction in the middle requires a simple change or a flying change.
  5. Ride the figure-eight 3-4 times, focusing on smooth changes of lead and consistent approaches.
  6. Each approach to a fence should feel the same: balanced canter, steady rhythm, looking ahead.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Rushing to the next fence without establishing a balanced canter first.
  • Forgetting to change leads, jumping the second fence on the wrong lead.
  • The turns becoming too tight or too wide, affecting the approach.
  • Losing confidence and checking (pulling) on the approach.

Variations & Progressions

  • Add a third fence in the middle of the figure-eight for a three-jump course.
  • Raise the fences to test confidence at height.
  • Ride the figure-eight in a specific stride count for precision.
Tip: The figure-eight is the simplest course pattern, but it tests everything: lead changes, approaches, rhythm, and track planning.
