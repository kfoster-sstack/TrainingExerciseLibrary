Riding in Open Spaces

Focus: Confidence, Control, Exposure

Equipment: Access to an open field

Arena Diagram

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. If you normally ride in an arena, take your horse to an open field or large paddock.
  2. Start at the walk. Let the horse look around and adjust to the larger space.
  3. Ride simple patterns: large circles, straight lines along fence lines.
  4. Progress to the trot after the horse is relaxed at the walk.
  5. If the horse becomes nervous, return to the walk and ride near the fence line for security.
  6. Gradually explore more of the open space. The goal is confident movement anywhere in the field.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Letting the horse gallop off because there's no arena fence to stop it.
  • Only riding along the fence line and never venturing into the middle of the field.
  • Pulling on the reins when nervous, which makes the horse more nervous.
  • Starting in too large an open space — a large paddock is better than an open field for beginners.

Variations & Progressions

  • Practice in progressively larger open spaces.
  • Ride in open spaces with a calm buddy horse for confidence.
  • Add canter work in the open field once the horse is relaxed at walk and trot.
Tip: Open spaces test your horse's training and your leadership. If you're confident, the horse will follow your lead.
