Flatwork / Dressage Intermediate 15 min

Halt-Trot Transitions

Focus: Transitions, Engagement, Obedience

Equipment: Letter markers

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. At the working trot, ride a 20-meter circle. Choose a specific point (cone or letter) for your transition.
  2. At your marker, transition directly to halt — no walk strides in between.
  3. The halt should be square and balanced. The horse should stand quietly for 3 seconds.
  4. From the halt, push directly into trot. Again, no walk strides.
  5. The trot should come forward and balanced, not rushed or running.
  6. Repeat 4-6 times around the circle. Each transition should be cleaner than the last.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Walk strides creeping into the transitions (either direction).
  • The halt being crooked, with the horse's body angled instead of straight.
  • The horse anticipating and halting before the marker.
  • The trot depart from halt being explosive instead of calm and forward.

Variations & Progressions

  • Progress to halt-canter transitions for a more advanced engagement exercise.
  • Perform halt-trot transitions on the center line for test preparation.
  • Add rein-back before the trot depart: halt, rein-back 3 steps, trot.
Tip: The quality of the halt predicts the quality of the depart. Take the time to establish a good halt before asking for trot.
