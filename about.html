About the Training Exercise Library

A free resource for trainers and riders, built by the Schneider Saddlery team.

Our Mission

The Training Exercise Library was created with a simple goal: to give every horse person, from the weekend trail rider to the competitive dressage professional, access to a well-organized collection of training exercises that actually work.

We believe that great training comes from great preparation. Having a library of proven exercises at your fingertips means you can plan purposeful rides, address specific training challenges, and keep your horse engaged and progressing.

Who We Are

The Training Exercise Library is a project of Schneider Saddlery, a family-owned equestrian retailer that has been serving riders since 1948. Based in Cleveland, Ohio, Schneider Saddlery has grown from a small leather shop to one of the most trusted names in the equine industry.

Our team includes riders and trainers across multiple disciplines who have curated, tested, and refined each exercise in this library. We also welcome contributions from the broader equestrian community through our exercise submission form.

How It Works

Every exercise in our library includes:

  • Clear discipline labeling so you can find exercises relevant to your riding style
  • Skill level ratings (beginner, intermediate, advanced) to match your horse's training stage
  • Time estimates so you can plan your ride effectively
  • Step-by-step instructions with 5-8 detailed steps for each exercise
  • Common mistakes to avoid so you do not accidentally train bad habits
  • Variations and progressions to keep challenging your horse as they improve
  • Equipment links connecting to sstack.com for anything you need to set up the exercise

Contributing

We encourage trainers, instructors, and experienced riders to submit their favorite training exercises. Submissions are reviewed by our editorial team for clarity, safety, and effectiveness before being added to the library. Visit the Submit an Exercise section on our main page to contribute.
