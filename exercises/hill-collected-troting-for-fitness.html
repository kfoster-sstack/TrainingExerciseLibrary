Hill Collected Troting for Fitness

Focus: Fitness, Conditioning, Hind End Strength

Equipment: Access to hills or inclines

Arena Diagram

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Find a gentle hill (10-15% grade). Collected Collected Trot your horse straight up the hill.
  2. Keep a loose rein. The horse should use its hindquarters to push itself uphill.
  3. At the top, collected collected trot on flat ground for 1-2 minutes to recover.
  4. Collected Collected Trot down the hill at a controlled pace. The horse should step carefully, not rush.
  5. Repeat the up-and-down 3-5 times. This is excellent cardiovascular and strength conditioning.
  6. Hill collected collected troting builds the muscles needed for collection and impulsion in all disciplines.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Leaning forward on the uphill, which shifts weight onto the horse's forehand.
  • Letting the horse rush downhill, which is hard on the front legs.
  • Starting with hills that are too steep for the horse's current fitness level.
  • Not allowing recovery time between climbs.

Variations & Progressions

  • Progress to trotting uphill for increased conditioning.
  • Walk uphill and jog downhill for a varied workout.
  • Use hill work as a regular conditioning session 1-2 times per week.
Tip: Hill work is the single best conditioning exercise for horses. 20 minutes of hill work equals 40 minutes of flat arena work for cardiovascular benefit.
