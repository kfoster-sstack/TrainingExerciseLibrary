Flatwork / Dressage Intermediate 20 min

Square Pattern

Focus: Accuracy, Straightness, Transitions

Equipment: 4 cones or markers

Arena Diagram

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Set up four cones in a large square, about 15 meters per side, centered in the arena.
  2. Starting at any cone, ride a perfectly straight line to the next cone.
  3. At each cone, make a precise 90-degree turn. The turn should be a quarter of a small circle, not a choppy right angle.
  4. Ride the entire square at the walk first, focusing on the quality of each straight segment and turn.
  5. Progress to the trot. At the trot, the turns become half-halts that require planning.
  6. Each straight segment should have consistent rhythm. Each turn should maintain balance.
  7. Ride the square in both directions for equal practice.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Cutting corners at the cones instead of riding precise 90-degree turns.
  • The straight segments curving instead of being truly straight.
  • The horse slowing excessively in the turns and then rushing on the straights.
  • Not looking ahead to the next cone, causing the horse to drift.

Variations & Progressions

  • Add transitions on the straight segments: trot one side, canter the next.
  • Decrease the square size to 10 meters for more demanding turns.
  • Add a halt at each cone: halt for 3 seconds, then proceed to the next side.
Tip: Think of the square as a test of your rein aids and leg aids working together. Each turn needs both.
