Canter Pirouette Preparation

Focus: Collection, Balance, Engagement

Equipment: 2 cones or markers

Arena Diagram

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Begin at the collected canter on a 20-meter circle. The canter should be very collected with active hind legs.
  2. Over 2-3 revolutions, decrease the circle size: 20 to 15 to 10 meters.
  3. On the 10-meter circle, the horse should be nearly performing a canter pirouette — the inside hind leg steps in a very small circle.
  4. Maintain the canter rhythm. The hind legs must keep the three-beat pattern.
  5. Hold the smallest circle for 2-3 strides, then spiral back out to 20 meters.
  6. The entire exercise should feel like a coiled spring: the smaller the circle, the more engaged the haunches.
  7. Walk and stretch. Pirouette work is extremely demanding on the horse's hind legs.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • The canter becoming four-beat as the circle shrinks (loss of collection).
  • The horse falling onto the inside shoulder instead of sitting on the inside hind.
  • Losing the canter entirely and breaking to trot on the small circle.
  • The rider leaning inward, throwing the horse off balance.

Variations & Progressions

  • Practice walk pirouettes first to establish the concept of turning on the haunches.
  • Work the canter spiral only down to 12-15 meters until the horse builds strength.
  • Add a flying change after spiraling out for a combination exercise.
Tip: Pirouette work is like strength training. Start with what your horse can do well and gradually ask for more. Never force a smaller circle than the horse can maintain quality.
