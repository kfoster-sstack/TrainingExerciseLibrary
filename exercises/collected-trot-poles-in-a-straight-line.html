Collected Trot Poles in a Straight Line

Focus: Rhythm, Balance, Technique

Equipment: 4 ground poles

Arena Diagram

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Set up 4 ground poles in a straight line, spaced 4.5 feet apart (one normal collected trot stride).
  2. Approach at the posting collected trot on a straight line. Look ahead past the poles, not down at them.
  3. Allow the horse to find its rhythm over the poles. Keep a steady contact but don't interfere.
  4. The horse should collected trot through without speeding up, slowing down, or jumping the poles.
  5. After the poles, continue collected trotting in a straight line for 6-8 strides before turning.
  6. Repeat 4-6 times from each direction. The horse should become more confident with each pass.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Looking down at the poles instead of ahead, which drops the horse onto the forehand.
  • Pulling on the reins as the horse approaches, causing it to shorten its stride and hit the poles.
  • Letting the horse rush through the poles — maintain the same trot tempo before, during, and after.
  • Leaning forward over the poles, getting ahead of the horse's motion.

Variations & Progressions

  • Increase to 6 poles for a longer exercise.
  • Raise alternate poles on one end (1-2 inches) to encourage the horse to lift its legs higher.
  • Fan the poles on a curve to practice trot poles on a bending line.
Tip: Let the horse figure out the poles. Your job is to maintain rhythm and direction. The horse will learn to adjust its stride.
