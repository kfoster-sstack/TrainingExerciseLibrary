Trail Etiquette Practice Ride at the Collected Trot

Focus: Safety, Manners, Trail Skills

Equipment: None required

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Practice the protocols for encountering other trail users while riding.
  2. Meeting hikers: halt your horse, greet the hiker verbally, and allow them to pass while your horse stands still.
  3. Meeting cyclists: stop, alert the cyclist that you're on a horse, and hold your horse steady as they pass.
  4. Meeting other horses: keep a safe distance, match the speed of the group, and don't rush past.
  5. Passing a horse going the same direction: call out, wait for acknowledgment, then pass at a collected collected trot.
  6. Practice these scenarios in the arena with friends playing the roles of hikers and cyclists.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Not halting for hikers or cyclists, which is dangerous for everyone.
  • Allowing your horse to crowd or nip at other horses on the trail.
  • Galloping past other trail users, which can cause spooking and accidents.
  • Not communicating with other trail users — always call out when approaching.

Variations & Progressions

  • Practice trail etiquette with dogs by having a friend bring a calm dog to the arena.
  • Simulate meeting a stroller or wheelchair for additional exposure.
  • Practice sharing narrow trail sections by riding past close objects in the arena.
Tip: Trail etiquette isn't just polite — it's safety. A horse that stands calmly while a bicycle passes is a horse that protects its rider.
