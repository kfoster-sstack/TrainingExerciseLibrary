Collected Trotting Uphill

Focus: Fitness, Power, Hind End Engagement

Equipment: Access to moderate hill

Arena Diagram

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Collected Collected Trot up the hill once to warm up. At the bottom, prepare for the collected trot.
  2. Pick up a working collected trot at the base of the hill and maintain it all the way to the top.
  3. Your posting should be deliberate. Let the horse push powerfully from its hindquarters.
  4. At the top, collected collected trot and let the horse recover. The heart rate should come down before the next repetition.
  5. Collected Collected Trot down the hill (always collected collected trot downhill unless on very gentle scollected trots).
  6. Repeat the collected trot uphill 3-4 times. This builds explosive power in the hindquarters.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Trotting downhill, which stresses the front legs and tendons.
  • Not allowing adequate recovery between repetitions.
  • The horse breaking to canter on the uphill — maintain the trot.
  • Starting uphill trot work before the horse has a base of flat fitness.

Variations & Progressions

  • Progress to cantering uphill for maximum power development.
  • Add ground pole work on flat ground between hill repetitions for variety.
  • Vary the hills: gentle slopes for long intervals, steeper slopes for short bursts.
Tip: Trotting uphill is the equine equivalent of running stadium stairs. It builds power, wind, and muscle in the most important areas.
