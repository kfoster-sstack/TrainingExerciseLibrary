Mounting and Dismounting from Both Sides

Focus: Flexibility, Safety, Independence

Equipment: Mounting block

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Most riders only mount from the left. This exercise trains mounting from both sides.
  2. Using a mounting block, mount from the left side as normal. Ride a circle, then dismount.
  3. Now reposition the mounting block on the right side of the horse.
  4. Mount from the right side. This will feel strange at first — your stirrup foot and hand positions are reversed.
  5. Ride a circle, then dismount from the right side.
  6. Practice until mounting from either side feels natural. On the trail, you may need to mount from the right.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Not using a mounting block (for the horse's back health).
  • The horse moving away when you attempt to mount from the unfamiliar right side.
  • Not adjusting stirrups from the right side (they may feel different).
  • Rushing the process — take time to figure out the reversed mechanics.

Variations & Progressions

  • Practice emergency dismounts from both sides.
  • Mount from uneven ground (uphill side vs. downhill side).
  • Practice mounting without a mounting block for trail emergencies.
Tip: Being able to mount from either side is a practical safety skill. On the trail, you may be next to a cliff on the left — you need to mount from the right.
