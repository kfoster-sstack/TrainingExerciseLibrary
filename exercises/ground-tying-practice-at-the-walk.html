Ground-Tying Practice at the Walk

Focus: Obedience, Patience, Independence

Equipment: Halter and leadrope

Arena Diagram

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Start with your horse tied normally. Untie the rope and drop it on the ground.
  2. Step 2 feet away from the horse. If it stands still for 5 seconds, step back and praise.
  3. Gradually increase the distance: 5 feet, 10 feet, 15 feet.
  4. If the horse moves, calmly return it to the exact spot and start over.
  5. Build up the duration: 30 seconds, 1 minute, 2 minutes at 15+ feet away.
  6. The horse should learn that a dropped rope means 'stand here and don't move.'

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Starting with too much distance or duration.
  • Getting angry when the horse moves — calmly return it every time.
  • Practicing in a distracting environment before the horse is solid in a quiet one.
  • Not returning the horse to the exact same spot, which muddies the expectation.

Variations & Progressions

  • Practice ground-tying during grooming (set the rope down while brushing).
  • Ground-tie while you walk to the tack room and back.
  • Ground-tie in progressively more challenging environments (near other horses, near the gate).
Tip: Ground-tying is the ultimate patience exercise. A horse that ground-ties has the mental discipline for advanced training.
