Jumping Position at the Canter and Canter

Focus: Technique, Balance, Fitness

Equipment: None required

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. At the canter, rise into two-point position: your seat lifts slightly off the saddle, weight drops into your heels.
  2. Hold the two-point position for 10 strides, then sit for 10 strides. Alternate.
  3. Your hands should stay steady over the horse's withers. Don't brace on the neck.
  4. Progress to the canter in two-point. This is harder because of the rocking motion.
  5. Hold two-point at the canter for 10 strides, then sit. Build up to 20, then 30 strides.
  6. This develops the leg strength and balance needed for jumping.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Gripping with the knees instead of keeping weight in the heels.
  • Resting hands on the horse's neck for balance instead of being self-supporting.
  • Collapsing the upper body forward instead of maintaining a flat back with eyes up.
  • Holding breath — keep breathing normally throughout.

Variations & Progressions

  • Hold two-point for an entire trot circle (30+ strides) for endurance.
  • Practice two-point while trotting over ground poles.
  • Add arm exercises in two-point: arms out to the side, arms forward, hands on helmet.
Tip: Two-point is the foundation of jumping position. If you can't hold it for 30 seconds at the canter, you're not ready for courses.
