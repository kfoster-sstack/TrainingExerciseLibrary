Flatwork / Dressage Beginner 15 min

Change of Rein Across the Diagonal

Focus: Straightness, Accuracy, Transitions

Equipment: 2 cones or markers

Arena Diagram

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Start tracking left at the working trot. As you approach the corner before the long side, look across the diagonal to M (or K).
  2. At the letter before the corner (F or H), begin turning across the diagonal toward the opposite corner letter.
  3. Ride a straight diagonal line. Your horse should be perfectly straight — no bend in either direction.
  4. As you approach the opposite track, prepare the new bend 2-3 strides before reaching the rail.
  5. Smoothly join the track on the new rein with the correct bend already established.
  6. Repeat 4-6 diagonal changes, alternating direction each time. Focus on the straightness of the diagonal line.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • The diagonal line curving instead of being perfectly straight.
  • Not preparing the new bend early enough, resulting in a clumsy arrival at the track.
  • The horse speeding up on the diagonal because it feels like open space.
  • Cutting the corner before the diagonal instead of riding deep into the corner.

Variations & Progressions

  • Perform the diagonal change at the canter, adding a simple change through trot at X.
  • Add a transition at X: trot to walk to trot, changing the posting diagonal.
  • Ride the diagonal in medium trot, collecting back to working trot at X.
Tip: The diagonal is your horse's straightness test. If you drift to one side, it reveals which shoulder is dominant.
