Flatwork / Dressage Beginner 15 min

Center Line Riding

Focus: Straightness, Accuracy, Precision

Equipment: Letter markers

Arena Diagram

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. At A, turn onto the center line heading toward C. Look straight ahead at C to guide your line.
  2. Your horse should be absolutely straight on the center line. Use both legs evenly to prevent drift.
  3. Check your straightness by glancing down at the line in the footing (if visible) or at the arena walls to ensure equal distance on both sides.
  4. Ride the full center line at the walk. At C, turn left or right onto the track.
  5. Repeat at the trot. Straightness becomes harder at faster gaits because any imbalance is magnified.
  6. Practice the center line 4-6 times. This is a fundamental skill for every dressage test entry.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Drifting to one side because the horse favors one shoulder over the other.
  • Looking down at the horse's neck instead of ahead at C.
  • The initial turn onto the center line being too shallow or too deep.
  • Losing straightness in the last 3-4 strides before C because the horse anticipates the turn.

Variations & Progressions

  • Add a halt at X for a halt-salute test preparation exercise.
  • Ride the center line in medium trot for added difficulty.
  • Practice the center line at the canter with a specific lead for test preparation.
Tip: The center line reveals the truth about your horse's straightness. Film it from C and you'll see every drift and wobble.
