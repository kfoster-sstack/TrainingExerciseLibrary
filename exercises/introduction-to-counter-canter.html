Flatwork / Dressage Intermediate 20 min

Introduction to Counter-Canter

Focus: Balance, Straightness, Collection

Equipment: 4 cones or markers

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Establish a balanced right-lead canter on the track.
  2. As you approach the short side, instead of changing leads, continue on the right lead through the turn.
  3. On the short side, you are now in counter-canter. Maintain a very slight right flexion (toward the lead).
  4. Ride one short side in counter-canter, then return to true canter on the long side.
  5. If the horse swaps leads, come back to trot, establish the correct lead, and try again.
  6. Gradually extend the counter-canter distance: one short side, then one full end of the arena, then the entire arena.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • The horse swapping leads because it assumes it should change.
  • Over-bending the neck toward the lead, destabilizing the balance.
  • The canter becoming four-beat from tension and loss of impulsion.
  • The rider gripping and becoming stiff, which transfers tension to the horse.

Variations & Progressions

  • Ride a shallow loop on the long side in counter-canter (come 3-5 meters off the rail and back).
  • Counter-canter on a 20-meter circle for advanced balance.
  • Use counter-canter as preparation for flying changes — it teaches the horse to wait for your aid.
Tip: Counter-canter should feel like normal canter with a slight lean. If it feels like a struggle, the horse needs more basic canter balance first.
