Flatwork / Dressage Beginner 15 min

Free Walk to Medium Walk Transition

Focus: Transitions, Relaxation, Contact

Equipment: Letter markers

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. At the free walk, allow maximum rein length. The horse should stretch its neck forward and down with long, ground-covering strides.
  2. 5-6 strides before your transition point, begin slowly taking up the reins.
  3. Shorten the reins gradually, not abruptly. The horse should accept the contact without raising its head sharply.
  4. Transition to medium walk: the horse shortens its frame but maintains energy and rhythm.
  5. The medium walk should feel purposeful and marching, not restricted or sluggish.
  6. Practice this transition 4-6 times. It appears in every dressage test and is often scored poorly.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Snatching up the reins abruptly, causing the horse to hollow its back.
  • The horse losing energy in the transition, going from a big free walk to a shuffling medium walk.
  • Not allowing enough strides for the transition — it should take 3-4 strides to smoothly take up the contact.
  • The horse raising its head and bracing against the new contact.

Variations & Progressions

  • Practice the reverse: medium walk to free walk, which is equally important in tests.
  • Add a trot transition immediately after establishing medium walk.
  • Use the free walk to medium walk as a test for throughness — a tense horse won't transition smoothly.
Tip: This transition is one of the most frequently penalized movements in dressage. Practice it until the rein-taking feels invisible.
