Two-Jump Bending Line

Focus: Accuracy, Adjustability, Track Planning

Equipment: 4 jump poles, 4 jump standards, 4 cones or markers

Arena Diagram

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Set up two small verticals about 60 feet apart on a bending line (not a straight line).
  2. The rider must arc between the two fences, choosing the path and number of strides.
  3. A direct line (less curve) might ride in 4 strides. A bending line (more curve) might ride in 5.
  4. Approach the first fence on a good canter. After landing, look at the second fence and plan your curve.
  5. Use your inside leg to maintain impulsion on the curve. Your outside rein guides the track.
  6. Practice both the 4-stride (direct) and 5-stride (bending) option to develop adjustability.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Not planning the line before jumping the first fence.
  • Drifting wide on the bending line, adding too many strides.
  • Cutting the line too tight and getting too close to the second fence.
  • Losing impulsion on the curve because the rider focuses only on direction.

Variations & Progressions

  • Set up the line with different options: 4, 5, or 6 strides depending on the path.
  • Ride the bending line in both directions for a figure-eight course.
  • Add a third fence after the bending line for course simulation.
Tip: Bending lines test your track-planning skills. Walk the line on foot first to see the different paths available.
