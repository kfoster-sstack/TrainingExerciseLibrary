Ranch Riding: Extended Canter

Focus: Impulsion, Freedom, Natural Movement

Equipment: None required

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Ranch riding values a natural, forward-moving horse. The extended canter should cover maximum ground.
  2. From the working canter, ask the horse to open its stride into an extended canter.
  3. Post the extended canter. The horse should reach forward with its front legs and push powerfully from behind.
  4. The frame should be natural — lower than dressage but active and forward.
  5. Ride the extended canter on the long sides, collecting back to working canter in the corners.
  6. The contrast between working canter and extended canter should be dramatic.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • The extended trot looking like a fast jog instead of a truly lengthened stride.
  • The horse becoming hollow and inverted during the extension.
  • Not enough difference between the working jog and extended trot.
  • Posting too fast, driving the horse faster instead of longer.

Variations & Progressions

  • Practice extended trot on the diagonal for a different spatial challenge.
  • Add transitions: working jog to extended trot to working jog every 20 strides.
  • Use ground poles to encourage genuine stride lengthening.
Tip: Ranch riding extended trot should look like a horse covering ground to get to the next pasture. Natural, ground-covering, and purposeful.
