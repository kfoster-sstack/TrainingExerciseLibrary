Backing in a Circle

Focus: Precision, Body Control, Collection

Equipment: 2 cones or markers

Arena Diagram

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Set up 2 cones about 10 feet apart. Position your horse between them.
  2. Ask the horse to back up while simultaneously steering its haunches to create a circular backing pattern.
  3. The horse should back in a small circle, its front end relatively still while the haunches describe the larger arc.
  4. Back half a circle (180 degrees), then walk forward and repeat in the other direction.
  5. This requires precise coordination of rein and leg aids — one hand steers, legs control the haunches.
  6. Build to a full 360-degree backing circle in each direction.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • The horse going straight back instead of curving.
  • Forward motion creeping in because the backing becomes confusing.
  • Only the forehand or haunches moving, not the entire horse in a curve.
  • The circle being too large — aim for a tight, controlled arc.

Variations & Progressions

  • Back through a U-shaped pole configuration.
  • Chain: back a circle left, walk forward, back a circle right.
  • Compete with friends: who can back the most accurate circle?
Tip: Backing in a circle is one of the most demanding exercises in western riding. It requires the horse to yield multiple body parts simultaneously while moving backward.
