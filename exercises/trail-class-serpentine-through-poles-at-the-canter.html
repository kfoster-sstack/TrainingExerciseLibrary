Western Intermediate 25 min

Trail Class: Serpentine Through Poles at the Canter

Focus: Accuracy, Steering, Patience

Equipment: 6 ground poles

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Set up 6 poles in a row, spaced 7 feet apart, perpendicular to the direction of travel.
  2. Canter through the poles in a serpentine pattern, passing each pole on alternating sides.
  3. The horse must cross over each pole while weaving left-right-left through the pattern.
  4. Maintain a steady canter rhythm. Don't rush or slow down between poles.
  5. Each turn should be smooth and flowing, not jerky.
  6. Practice in both directions for equal development.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • The horse stepping on poles instead of cleanly crossing over them.
  • Losing the serpentine pattern and going straight through.
  • The jog becoming irregular as the horse navigates the turns and poles.
  • Not planning 2 poles ahead, causing late turns and missed poles.

Variations & Progressions

  • Lope the serpentine for advanced riders.
  • Decrease pole spacing to 6 feet for tighter turns.
  • Walk the serpentine backward for a challenging variation.
Tip: The serpentine through poles combines two skills: steering accuracy and pole awareness. Master each separately before combining.
