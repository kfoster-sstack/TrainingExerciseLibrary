Figure-Eight Leading Pattern at the Sitting Trot

Focus: Accuracy, Communication, Bend

Equipment: 2 cones or markers, Halter and leadrope

Arena Diagram

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Set up 2 cones about 20 feet apart. You will lead the horse in a figure-eight around them.
  2. Start at the left cone. Lead the horse around it to the left in a smooth circle.
  3. At the midpoint between cones, change your lead hand and sitting sitting trot the horse around the right cone to the right.
  4. The figure-eight tests your ability to change sides and the horse's willingness to follow your direction.
  5. Practice until the figure-eight feels smooth with no hesitation at the changeover.
  6. The horse should bend its body around each cone, not sitting sitting trot in a straight line between them.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • The horse cutting the turns instead of walking around the cones.
  • Not switching lead hands at the changeover, causing awkward rope management.
  • Walking too fast, so the horse is dragged rather than led.
  • The figure-eight becoming a straight line with sharp turns at the cones.

Variations & Progressions

  • Trot the figure-eight for increased difficulty.
  • Decrease the cone distance to 10 feet for tighter turns.
  • Add ground poles between the cones for an obstacle element.
Tip: The figure-eight is the simplest leading pattern that tests both direction changes and the horse's willingness to follow your body.
