Western Beginner 15 min

Trail Class: Back Through a Chute at the Walk

Focus: Precision, Patience, Body Control

Equipment: 6 ground poles

Arena Diagram

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Set up a chute using ground poles: two parallel lines about 4 feet apart, 15 feet long.
  2. Walk your horse forward through the chute to show it the path.
  3. At the end of the chute, halt. Ask the horse to back through the chute.
  4. Guide with light rein pressure. The horse should back in a straight line without stepping on the poles.
  5. Keep the horse's body straight. If the haunches drift, correct with leg pressure.
  6. Back through the entire chute, then walk forward to reward.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • The horse's haunches drifting and stepping outside the chute.
  • Rushing the back-up instead of taking it one step at a time.
  • Using too much rein and not enough leg to guide the horse straight.
  • The horse refusing to back because it can't see where it's going.

Variations & Progressions

  • Narrow the chute to 3 feet for increased precision.
  • Add an L-turn in the chute for a more complex pattern.
  • Practice backing through different chute configurations (straight, L, U-shape).
Tip: Backing through a chute requires trust. The horse can't see where it's going, so it relies on your aids. Be clear and consistent.
