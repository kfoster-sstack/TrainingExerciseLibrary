Terrain Variety Walk at the Walk

Focus: Confidence, Balance, Exposure

Equipment: Access to varied terrain

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Take your horse on a walk through as many different surfaces as possible.
  2. Include: grass, gravel, sand, pavement, dirt, leaves, and any available surface changes.
  3. At each new surface, allow the horse to investigate by lowering its head.
  4. Walk calmly across each surface. If the horse hesitates, wait and encourage gently.
  5. The goal is a horse that walks confidently across any surface without spooking.
  6. Spend 20-30 minutes exploring different terrain types.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Rushing from one surface to the next without letting the horse adjust.
  • Forcing the horse onto surfaces it's genuinely frightened of (approach gradually).
  • Not riding or leading on a loose rein, which prevents the horse from balancing naturally.
  • Only training on one surface type (arena footing) and never exposing the horse to variety.

Variations & Progressions

  • Trot across different surfaces once the walk is confident.
  • Ride over terrain changes for a mounted version.
  • Combine with hill work for a comprehensive trail preparation session.
Tip: A trail horse needs to handle any footing. The more surfaces you practice at home, the fewer surprises on the trail.
