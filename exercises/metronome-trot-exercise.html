Flatwork / Dressage Beginner 15 min

Metronome Trot Exercise

Focus: Rhythm, Tempo, Consistency

Equipment: None required

Arena Diagram

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Choose a working trot tempo. Count the beats in your head: 'one-two, one-two' for trot.
  2. Ride the entire arena perimeter at this exact tempo. Use the long sides to check consistency.
  3. If you have a metronome app, set it to your trot tempo and ride to the beat.
  4. Notice where the tempo changes: typically in corners (slower) and on long sides (faster).
  5. Adjust your aids to maintain the same tempo everywhere: use more leg in corners, half-halt on long sides.
  6. Ride for 5 minutes at the exact same tempo, then walk and rest.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Speeding up on the long side because the horse has more room.
  • Slowing down in corners because the bend disrupts the rhythm.
  • Confusing rhythm (the beat) with speed (how fast the horse travels).
  • Changing posting speed, which changes the horse's tempo.

Variations & Progressions

  • Perform the metronome exercise at the canter for three-beat rhythm work.
  • Add transitions while maintaining the same underlying tempo intention.
  • Practice at different tempos: a slower working trot vs. a more forward working trot.
Tip: Rhythm is the first pillar of the training scale for a reason. Without consistent rhythm, nothing else works.
