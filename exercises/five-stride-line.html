Jumping Intermediate 25 min

Five-Stride Line

Focus: Stride Control, Rhythm, Accuracy

Equipment: 4 jump poles, 4 jump standards

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Set up 2 fences 72 feet apart (five normal canter strides).
  2. Approach at the working canter. After the first fence, count: 1-2-3-4-5.
  3. You should arrive at the second fence in a comfortable distance on stride 5.
  4. If you get 6 strides, your canter needs more forward energy. If you get 4, collect slightly.
  5. Once you can reliably get 5 strides, try 6 (collected) and 4 (open).
  6. The ability to ride the same line in different stride counts is the mark of a skilled rider.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Losing count and guessing at the second fence.
  • Pulling to add the extra stride instead of using your seat to collect.
  • Running to leave out a stride, arriving dangerously close to the second fence.
  • Not maintaining a consistent path between the fences.

Variations & Progressions

  • Practice both 4 and 6 strides in the same session for adjustability contrast.
  • Set up two five-stride lines at different angles for a mini-course.
  • Time yourself to see which stride count is fastest (hint: fewer strides, straight track).
Tip: Five strides is a common show jumping distance. Know your horse's normal stride and you can add or leave out with confidence.
