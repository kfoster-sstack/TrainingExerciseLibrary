Jumping Beginner 15 min

Bounce Poles at the Trot

Focus: Technique, Rhythm, Confidence

Equipment: 3 ground poles

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Set up 3 ground poles spaced 3 feet apart (bounce distance at the trot).
  2. Approach at a steady sitting trot. The horse will take quick, bouncy steps over each pole.
  3. Keep your contact steady and your upper body slightly behind vertical to support the horse.
  4. The horse should not have a full stride between poles — it bounces from one to the next.
  5. This exercise improves the horse's hind leg engagement and front leg technique.
  6. Repeat 4-6 times. The horse should get smoother with each pass.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Approaching too fast, causing the horse to jump the poles instead of bouncing.
  • The horse stopping or running out because the close spacing surprises it.
  • The rider getting ahead of the motion and landing on the horse's neck.
  • Not enough impulsion, causing the horse to shuffle through lazily.

Variations & Progressions

  • Increase to 4-5 bounce poles for a longer exercise.
  • Raise the poles to cavaletti height (6-8 inches) for more engagement.
  • Follow the bounce poles with a small cross-rail 9 feet after the last pole.
Tip: Bounce poles are gymnastics for horses. They develop the quick reflexes and hind leg engagement needed for jumping.
