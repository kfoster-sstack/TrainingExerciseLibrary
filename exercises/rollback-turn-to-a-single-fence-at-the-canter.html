Rollback Turn to a Single Fence at the Canter

Focus: Agility, Balance, Track Planning

Equipment: 2 jump poles, 2 jump standards

Arena Diagram

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Set up a single vertical in the center of the arena.
  2. Jump the fence, then immediately ride a tight rollback turn (half-circle) and jump it again from the other direction.
  3. The rollback should be a smooth half-circle, not a sharp pivot. Maintain the canter throughout.
  4. Start with a wide rollback (20-meter half-circle) and gradually tighten to 10-15 meters.
  5. The horse needs to land, rebalance on the turn, and set up for the next approach in just a few strides.
  6. Practice rollbacks to both directions for even development.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • The turn being too tight, causing the horse to break to trot or lose balance.
  • Not looking early enough in the direction of the rollback turn.
  • Landing and running straight for too many strides before starting the turn.
  • The second approach being rushed and unbalanced.

Variations & Progressions

  • Set up two fences and alternate rollback turns between them.
  • Tighten the rollback to simulate jump-off turns.
  • Add a related distance after the rollback: rollback to two-stride line.
Tip: Rollbacks win jump-offs. The tighter your rollback, the fewer strides you waste. But tightness should never sacrifice balance.
