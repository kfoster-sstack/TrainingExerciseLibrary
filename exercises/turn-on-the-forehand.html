Flatwork / Dressage Beginner 15 min

Turn on the Forehand

Focus: Lateral Work, Responsiveness, Obedience

Equipment: None required

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Halt your horse on the center line or along the rail. Establish a square, balanced halt.
  2. To turn on the forehand to the right, apply your left leg behind the girth while keeping a steady contact.
  3. The horse's front legs should remain almost stationary, stepping in a small circle, while the hind legs cross over and around.
  4. Your right leg stays passive near the girth to prevent the horse from walking forward.
  5. Your reins keep a light, even contact to prevent forward movement without pulling backward.
  6. Ask for one step at a time. Reward each correct step before asking for the next.
  7. Complete a 180-degree turn (half-turn on the forehand), then walk forward. Repeat in the other direction.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • The horse walking forward through the turn instead of pivoting on the front legs.
  • Using too much rein and causing the horse to step backward.
  • Asking for too many steps at once, causing the horse to rush and lose coordination.
  • The front legs walking a large circle instead of staying nearly in place.

Variations & Progressions

  • Progress to a full 360-degree turn on the forehand.
  • Practice from the halt and from the walk for different levels of difficulty.
  • Use the turn on the forehand as a gate-opening exercise: approach a gate, turn on the forehand to position yourself parallel to it.
Tip: This is a building block for all lateral work. If your horse understands moving away from leg pressure, every lateral movement becomes easier.
