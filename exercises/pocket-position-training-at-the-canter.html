Pocket Position Training at the Canter

Focus: Positioning, Accuracy, Turn Quality

Equipment: 1 barrel, 4 cones or markers

Arena Diagram

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. The pocket is the space between you and the barrel at the start of the turn. Set up a barrel with a cone 4 feet from it.
  2. Approach the barrel. When your horse's nose reaches the cone, begin the turn.
  3. The cone represents where your horse should be when starting the turn — not too close, not too far.
  4. Too close (inside the cone) risks knocking the barrel. Too far (outside the cone) creates a wide turn.
  5. Practice approaching and hitting the pocket consistently from both approach angles.
  6. Move the cone closer (3 feet) or farther (5 feet) to adjust the pocket for your horse's size and turn style.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Approaching too tight and not leaving enough room for the horse's body in the turn.
  • Approaching too wide and wasting time on a big, looping turn.
  • Not being consistent — the pocket should be the same distance every run.
  • Forgetting that different barrels may need different pocket distances based on approach angle.

Variations & Progressions

  • Mark your pocket with a consistent visual landmark for each barrel.
  • Practice the pocket at different speeds to maintain consistency.
  • Have someone stand at the pocket distance and tell you if you're hitting it.
Tip: The pocket is where the race is won or lost. A rider who hits the same pocket every time is a rider who wins consistently.
