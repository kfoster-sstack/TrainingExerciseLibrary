Neck Reining on the Trail at the Canter

Focus: Steering, Responsiveness, Trail Skills

Equipment: None required

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. On a trail or open area, practice one-handed steering with neck rein cues.
  2. Navigate around trees, bushes, and natural obstacles using neck rein only.
  3. Your free hand should be available for tree branches, gate latches, or carrying items.
  4. Practice turns in both directions. Most trail situations require quick, one-handed steering.
  5. Ride a serpentine through natural obstacles for practice.
  6. This is a practical skill — on the trail, you often need one hand free.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Reverting to two-handed riding when the steering gets challenging.
  • The horse not responding to neck rein because it was only trained with direct rein.
  • Not practicing neck reining in the arena before taking it to the trail.
  • Pulling too aggressively with the neck rein, causing the horse to over-turn.

Variations & Progressions

  • Practice neck reining at the trot on the trail for increased difficulty.
  • Open and close real gates using neck rein to position the horse.
  • Navigate through tight spaces (between trees) using only neck rein cues.
Tip: Trail riding is where neck reining proves its worth. A horse that neck reins smoothly is a joy on the trail.
