Jumping Intermediate 30 min

Four-Fence Mini Course

Focus: Track Planning, Rhythm, Confidence

Equipment: 8 jump poles, 8 jump standards

Arena Diagram

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Set up 4 small fences in a pattern that requires turns and changes of direction.
  2. Walk the course on foot first: plan your track, count your strides between fences, identify your lead changes.
  3. Start with fence 1. After landing, plan your turn to fence 2. Focus on a balanced approach.
  4. After fence 2, adjust your canter for the distance to fence 3. Look ahead to plan your track.
  5. Fence 3 to fence 4 completes the course. Finish with a controlled canter after the last fence.
  6. Debrief: were your distances right? Were your turns smooth? Were your leads correct?
  7. Repeat the course 2-3 times, improving each element with every pass.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Not walking the course on foot before riding it.
  • Getting lost mid-course because you didn't memorize the pattern.
  • Rushing between fences instead of re-establishing rhythm.
  • Only focusing on the current fence instead of planning 2 fences ahead.

Variations & Progressions

  • Raise the fences for confidence building at height.
  • Add a fifth and sixth fence for a more complex course.
  • Time the course and try to ride it more efficiently (not faster, but smoother).
Tip: Always think two fences ahead. While you're jumping fence 1, you should already be planning fence 2 and looking toward fence 3.
