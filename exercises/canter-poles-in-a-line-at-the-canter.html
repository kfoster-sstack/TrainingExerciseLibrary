Canter Poles in a Line at the Canter

Focus: Rhythm, Balance, Stride Control

Equipment: 4 ground poles

Arena Diagram

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Set up 4 ground poles spaced 9 feet apart (one normal canter stride between each).
  2. Approach at the working canter. Establish a steady rhythm 6-8 strides before the poles.
  3. Look ahead past the poles. Keep your leg on to maintain impulsion through the poles.
  4. The horse should canter through with one stride between each pole without chipping in extra strides.
  5. After the poles, maintain the canter for 6-8 strides before transitioning or turning.
  6. Repeat from both leads. Note if the horse is more comfortable on one lead.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • The horse breaking to trot before or during the poles.
  • Chipping in an extra stride between poles because the canter is too short.
  • The horse jumping the poles instead of cantering over them.
  • The rider throwing their upper body forward at each pole.

Variations & Progressions

  • Increase the spacing to 10 feet to encourage a longer stride.
  • Decrease to 8 feet to develop collection and adjustability.
  • Raise the poles to 6 inches to create low cavaletti work.
Tip: If the horse adds strides, the canter is too slow. If it leaves out strides, the canter is too fast. Adjust the canter, not the pole spacing.
