Flatwork / Dressage Intermediate 15 min

Quarter-Line Riding

Focus: Straightness, Balance, Independence

Equipment: 2 cones or markers

Arena Diagram

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. The quarter line is 5 meters from the rail. Place cones at each end to mark the line.
  2. Turn off the track and ride down the quarter line at the trot.
  3. Without the rail to guide you, your horse's natural drift becomes apparent.
  4. Use equal leg pressure to keep the horse straight. If it drifts toward the rail, apply inside leg. If it drifts inward, apply outside leg.
  5. Ride the quarter line in both directions on both reins.
  6. This exercise develops rider independence — you can't rely on the wall for straightness.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • The horse consistently drifting toward the rail for security.
  • Over-correcting and zigzagging instead of riding a straight line.
  • Not riding deep enough into the corner before turning onto the quarter line.
  • Losing the rhythm because you're focused on straightness.

Variations & Progressions

  • Ride the quarter line at the canter for increased difficulty.
  • Add transitions on the quarter line: trot-walk-trot without drifting.
  • Ride shoulder-fore on the quarter line to practice lateral positioning away from the rail.
Tip: If your horse drifts toward the rail, it's telling you which shoulder is dominant. Use this information to plan your training.
