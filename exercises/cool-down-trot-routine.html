Cool-Down Trot Routine

Focus: Cool-down, Recovery, Relaxation

Equipment: None required

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. After any workout, trot for a minimum of 10 minutes to cool the horse down.
  2. Allow a completely loose rein. The horse should stretch its neck down and out.
  3. Trot the entire arena perimeter at a relaxed pace.
  4. The horse's breathing should return to normal within the first 5 minutes.
  5. Check the horse for sweat patterns — even sweat indicates equal work on both sides.
  6. The cool-down trot is also your opportunity to mentally review the session.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Stopping work and immediately untacking without a cool-down.
  • Keeping the horse on a tight rein during the cool-down, preventing relaxation.
  • Cool-down being too short (under 5 minutes).
  • Dismounting and hand-walking only — mounted cool-down is important for the horse's back.

Variations & Progressions

  • Add gentle stretches (carrot stretches) after dismounting.
  • Cool down on the trail or in an open area for variety.
  • Include a few minutes of free walk on a long rein before coming to a halt.
Tip: The cool-down is as important as the workout. A proper cool-down reduces muscle soreness and helps the horse process the lesson.
