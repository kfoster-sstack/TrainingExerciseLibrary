Lope Departure from the Canter

Focus: Transitions, Responsiveness, Lead Control

Equipment: 4 cones or markers

Arena Diagram

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. At the canter, prepare for a canter departure. Sit deep and collect the canter slightly.
  2. Apply your outside leg behind the girth, inside leg at the girth, and cluck.
  3. The horse should step directly into the canter without canterging first.
  4. Canter for half the arena, then transition back to canter (no canter in between).
  5. Practice 4-6 departures on each lead. Each one should get cleaner.
  6. For western classes, the canter departure should be smooth and quiet, not explosive.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • The horse jogging before the lope, indicating unclear aids or insufficient collection.
  • Picking up the wrong lead because the outside leg position wasn't clear.
  • The departure being too abrupt or fast for western pleasure standards.
  • Not sitting deep enough during the departure, encouraging the horse to rush.

Variations & Progressions

  • Practice lope departures at specific cones for precision.
  • Add halt-lope transitions for more advanced responsiveness.
  • Work on lope departures on a circle for consistent leads.
Tip: A western lope departure should be invisible to spectators. If you can't tell when the horse changed gaits, it's a good departure.
