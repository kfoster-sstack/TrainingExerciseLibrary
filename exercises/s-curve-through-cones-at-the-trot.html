Flatwork / Dressage Beginner 15 min

S-Curve Through Cones at the Trot

Focus: Suppleness, Bend, Accuracy

Equipment: 6 cones or markers

Arena Diagram

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Set up 6 cones in a straight line down the center of the arena, spaced about 10 meters apart.
  2. Starting at the first cone, weave through the line in an S-curve pattern, passing each cone on alternating sides.
  3. At the trot, focus on smooth changes of bend as you curve around each cone.
  4. Your inside leg pushes the horse around each cone. Your outside leg supports and prevents the haunches from swinging.
  5. Progress to the trot once the trot feels smooth. The S-curve becomes more challenging at speed.
  6. Repeat the weave 3-4 times in each direction.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Cutting too close to the cones and knocking them over.
  • Making sharp turns instead of smooth, flowing S-curves.
  • Not establishing the new bend early enough, resulting in flat spots between curves.
  • The horse rushing through the pattern instead of maintaining a steady tempo.

Variations & Progressions

  • Move the cones closer together (8 meters) for tighter turns.
  • Ride the S-curve at the canter for advanced suppleness work.
  • Add a halt at every other cone for transition practice within the pattern.
Tip: Think of your horse as a river flowing between rocks. The path should be smooth and continuous, not jagged.
