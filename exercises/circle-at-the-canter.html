Flatwork / Dressage Intermediate 20 min

Circle at the Canter

Focus: Balance, Bend, Rhythm

Equipment: 4 cones or markers

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Begin at the working canter on a 20-meter circle. Establish a clear three-beat rhythm before focusing on accuracy.
  2. Place four cones as quarter-point guides. Your horse should pass directly over each cone.
  3. Sit deep in the saddle with your inside hip slightly forward. Your inside leg at the girth maintains impulsion while the outside leg behind the girth keeps the haunches on the track.
  4. Focus on maintaining an even bend: you should see just the inside eye and nostril. The outside rein provides half-halts to balance the canter.
  5. Ride 4-5 circles, then perform a downward transition to trot for 3-4 strides, and pick up canter again on the circle.
  6. Change direction through trot, pick up the new lead, and repeat the circle work on the other rein.
  7. Finish with a stretchy trot circle, allowing the horse to lower the neck and reach forward-downward.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • The horse breaking to trot because the rider is too tight with the reins or not supporting with the inside leg.
  • The canter becoming four-beat (lateral) due to the horse not being forward enough into the contact.
  • The circle drifting to one side because the rider doesn't correct with the outside aids.
  • Cross-cantering when picking up the new lead after a change of direction.

Variations & Progressions

  • Ride 15-meter canter circles requiring more collection and balance.
  • Add counter-canter on the circle for one revolution before returning to true canter.
  • Perform simple changes through walk at X when changing direction on a figure-eight.
Tip: If the canter feels rushed, try a quiet 'and-one-two-three' count in your head to set the rhythm you want.
