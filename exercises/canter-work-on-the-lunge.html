Canter Work on the Lunge

Focus: Fitness, Balance, Lead Control

Equipment: Lunge line, Lunge whip

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Warm up at the walk and trot for 5-7 minutes on each rein.
  2. To ask for canter, use a sharp voice command ('canter!') and flick the whip toward the haunches with more energy.
  3. The horse should pick up the correct lead (inside lead for the direction of the circle).
  4. Allow 2-3 minutes of canter in each direction. The circle should remain consistent in size.
  5. Transition back to trot with a calming voice ('trot') and a gentle check on the lunge line.
  6. If the horse picks up the wrong lead, bring back to trot and try again with clearer aids.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • The horse picking up the wrong lead because the handler's body position isn't clear.
  • The circle becoming very small at the canter because the horse is unbalanced.
  • Not enough warm-up before canter work, risking strain.
  • Cantering for too long on the lunge, which is very stressful on the inside legs.

Variations & Progressions

  • Add canter-trot-canter transitions on the lunge.
  • Place a single ground pole on the circle to canter over.
  • Work toward lengthening and shortening the canter stride on the lunge line.
Tip: Limit canter work on the lunge to 3-4 minutes per direction maximum. The constant curve stresses the inside joints.
