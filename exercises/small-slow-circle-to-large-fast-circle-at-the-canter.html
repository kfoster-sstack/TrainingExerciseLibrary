Western Intermediate 30 min

Small Slow Circle to Large Fast Circle at the Canter

Focus: Adjustability, Speed Control, Collection

Equipment: 4 cones or markers

Arena Diagram

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Start at a collected canter on a small circle (20 feet diameter) near the center of the arena.
  2. The small circle should be slow, collected, and precise. The horse's hind legs are well underneath.
  3. After 2-3 small circles, gradually open to a large circle (full arena width) at a faster canter.
  4. The large circle should cover more ground with longer, more forward strides.
  5. After 2 large circles, gradually close back to the small slow circle.
  6. The contrast between small/slow and large/fast demonstrates your horse's adjustability.
  7. This is a required element in reining patterns.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • The small circle not being truly slow and collected — the horse needs to sit and shorten.
  • The large circle not being truly fast — the horse needs to really open up its stride.
  • Not enough contrast between the two circle sizes and speeds.
  • The circles being misshapen (egg-shaped) instead of round.

Variations & Progressions

  • Add more speed contrast: very slow small circle vs. very fast large circle.
  • Practice the transition from small to large and back within 2 strides for quickness.
  • Include this exercise in a full reining pattern practice.
Tip: Reining judges score the difference between your small slow circles and large fast circles. The more contrast, the higher the score.
