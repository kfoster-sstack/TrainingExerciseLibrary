Bank Step-Up and Step-Down

Focus: Confidence, Balance, Athleticism

Equipment: Small bank or step (6-12 inches)

Arena Diagram

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Find or create a small bank (6-12 inch step up). Approach at the walk.
  2. Let the horse see the bank. Encourage it to step up with a cluck and leg pressure.
  3. The horse should step up calmly, not jump. Walk forward 3-4 strides after stepping up.
  4. Turn around and approach the bank from above (step-down). This is usually scarier.
  5. Allow the horse to pause at the edge, look down, and then step off deliberately.
  6. Practice both up and down 4-6 times until the horse is relaxed in both directions.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • The horse jumping up the bank instead of stepping, which can be unbalancing.
  • Not allowing the horse to look at the step-down before committing.
  • Going too fast, not giving the horse time to balance itself.
  • Starting with a bank that's too high for the horse's confidence level.

Variations & Progressions

  • Progress to larger banks (18-24 inches) as confidence grows.
  • Trot up and walk down for a more advanced exercise.
  • Combine bank work with water: step down into water.
Tip: Banks appear on every cross-country course. A horse comfortable with small banks at home will handle course banks confidently.
