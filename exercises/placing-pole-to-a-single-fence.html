Jumping Beginner 20 min

Placing Pole to a Single Fence

Focus: Technique, Confidence, Rhythm

Equipment: 3 jump poles, 2 jump standards

Arena Diagram

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Set up a small vertical with a placing pole 9 feet in front of it (one trot stride).
  2. Approach at the trot. The placing pole ensures you arrive at the correct takeoff distance every time.
  3. Trot over the placing pole. The horse will naturally take one stride and jump the fence.
  4. Focus on your position: heels down, eyes up, following hands, soft seat.
  5. The consistency of the placing pole allows you to work on your form without worrying about the distance.
  6. Repeat 6-8 times, gradually raising the fence as confidence grows.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Approaching in canter instead of trot, which changes the placing pole distance.
  • Looking down at the placing pole instead of at the fence.
  • Tensing up and grabbing the mane over the fence.
  • Not keeping enough leg on the approach, causing the horse to stop.

Variations & Progressions

  • Move the placing pole to 18 feet for a canter approach with one stride.
  • Remove the placing pole after several successful attempts to test your independent judgment.
  • Add a second fence one stride after the first for a gymnastic sequence.
Tip: Placing poles are training wheels for distance. Use them liberally, then gradually remove them as your eye develops.
