Canter Rhythm and Cadence

Focus: Rhythm, Relaxation, Collection

Equipment: None required

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. At the canter, focus on finding the slowest possible rhythm while maintaining forward motion.
  2. The western canter should be slow enough that you can sit comfortably without bouncing.
  3. Your hips should follow the horse's back in a gentle rocking motion. Relax your lower back.
  4. Ride the entire arena at the canter, counting the cadence: 'one-two, one-two.'
  5. The canter should be consistent everywhere: long sides, short sides, corners.
  6. If the horse speeds up, use a gentle 'whoa' and half-halt to reset the rhythm.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • The jog being too fast for comfortable sitting — slow it down gradually.
  • The rider bouncing because they're stiff in the lower back and hips.
  • The horse breaking to walk because the jog is too slow to maintain.
  • Using too much rein to maintain the slow jog, creating a tense horse.

Variations & Progressions

  • Practice the jog on a circle for consistent rhythm through turns.
  • Add jog transitions: walk-jog-walk every 20 strides for crispness.
  • Close your eyes for 10 strides to feel the jog rhythm in your body.
Tip: The ideal western jog feels like sitting in a rocking chair. If you're bouncing, the jog is too fast or your body is too tense.
