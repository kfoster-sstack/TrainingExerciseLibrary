Three-Stride Related Distance at the Sitting Trot

Focus: Stride Control, Rhythm, Confidence

Equipment: 4 jump poles, 4 jump standards

Arena Diagram

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Set up 2 small fences 48 feet apart (three normal sitting trot strides).
  2. Approach at the working sitting trot. After the first fence, count your strides: 1-2-3.
  3. You should arrive at the second fence with a comfortable distance on stride 3.
  4. Keep your leg on and your eyes up. Let the sitting trot rhythm carry you to the fence.
  5. If you get 4 strides, your sitting trot is too slow. If you get 2, it's too fast.
  6. Repeat 4-6 times until the 3-stride line feels automatic.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Counting strides wrong — start counting when the horse's front feet land after the first fence.
  • Changing the canter speed between the fences instead of maintaining rhythm.
  • Looking down between the fences instead of at the second fence.
  • Tensing up after the first fence because you're worried about the distance.

Variations & Progressions

  • Try the 3-stride in 4 strides (collected) or 2 strides (open) for adjustability.
  • Add a third fence for a 3-stride to 3-stride exercise.
  • Approach from trot for a different first-fence dynamic.
Tip: Three strides gives you plenty of time to adjust. If you maintain rhythm, the distance takes care of itself.
