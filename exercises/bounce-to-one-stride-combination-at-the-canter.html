Bounce to One-Stride Combination at the Canter

Focus: Technique, Power, Adjustability

Equipment: 6 jump poles, 6 jump standards, 1 ground pole

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Set up: placing pole, then cross-rail, then 10 feet to a second cross-rail (bounce), then 18 feet to a vertical (one stride).
  2. Approach at the canter. The placing pole sets up the first jump.
  3. Bounce the first two fences, then the horse has one stride to the third fence.
  4. The bounce develops quickness; the one-stride develops scope and bascule.
  5. Repeat 4-6 times, gradually raising the fences as the horse gains confidence.
  6. If the horse struggles, lower everything and rebuild confidence.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • The horse rushing after the bounce, arriving too close to the third fence.
  • Not enough impulsion to clear the third fence after the demanding bounce.
  • The rider getting ahead of the motion in the bounce and interfering with the one-stride.
  • Setting the fences too high before the horse is ready for the combination.

Variations & Progressions

  • Extend to bounce-one stride-one stride for a longer exercise.
  • Add an oxer as the final fence for scope development.
  • Change the one-stride distance to test adjustability: 17 feet vs. 19 feet.
Tip: This is a classic gymnastic combination. The bounce teaches quick feet; the one-stride teaches the horse to push and stretch.
