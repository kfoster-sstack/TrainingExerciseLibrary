Flatwork / Dressage Beginner 15 min

Leg Yield from Center Line to Wall at the Trot

Focus: Lateral Work, Responsiveness, Balance

Equipment: 2 cones or markers

Arena Diagram

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Start at A on the center line at the working trot.
  2. Ride straight for 3-4 strides to establish rhythm and straightness on the center line.
  3. Apply your left leg at the girth to leg yield to the right, moving the horse sideways toward the right wall.
  4. Keep a slight left flexion (away from the direction of sideways travel). The horse's body should be nearly straight.
  5. Maintain the forward tempo. The horse should reach the rail by the time you arrive at B or E.
  6. Trot, change direction, and repeat from the center line to the other wall.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Starting the leg yield before establishing straightness on the center line.
  • Too much neck bend, causing the shoulder to lead excessively.
  • The horse not reaching the wall, indicating insufficient lateral response to the leg aid.
  • Losing forward impulsion so the horse shuffles sideways without marching forward.

Variations & Progressions

  • Vary where you start the leg yield: start at C for a longer lateral distance.
  • Add a circle at B or E before completing the leg yield to the wall.
  • Increase speed to medium trot while maintaining the leg yield angle.
Tip: Start with just a few sideways steps and build up. Three good steps sideways are better than ten crooked ones.
