Groundwork Beginner 10 min

Backing Up from the Ground at the Walk

Focus: Respect, Obedience, Collection

Equipment: Halter and leadrope

Arena Diagram

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Stand facing your horse's chest. Hold the lead rope about 18 inches from the clip.
  2. Apply light backward pressure on the lead rope while saying 'back.' Wiggle the rope if needed.
  3. The moment the horse takes one step backward, release all pressure immediately. Praise.
  4. Ask for another step. Release and praise. Build to 3-4 steps in a row.
  5. The horse should step backward in diagonal pairs (like the rein-back under saddle).
  6. Practice until the horse backs from a light verbal command with minimal rope pressure.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Pulling continuously on the rope instead of using pressure-and-release.
  • Not releasing quickly enough when the horse responds, so it doesn't understand the reward.
  • Asking for too many steps at once before the horse understands the exercise.
  • Standing too close to the horse's front feet while asking for backing.

Variations & Progressions

  • Back your horse through an L-shape or around a cone for steering while backing.
  • Back uphill for strength building.
  • Use backing as a correction tool: horse walks ahead of you? Back it up 5 steps.
Tip: Backing builds respect because it asks the horse to yield space to you. A horse that backs readily respects your space.
