Flatwork / Dressage Beginner 10 min

Stretching Forward and Down at Walk

Focus: Suppleness, Relaxation, Topline

Equipment: None required

Arena Diagram

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Begin at a medium walk with a steady contact.
  2. Gradually let the reins slide through your fingers, offering 2-3 inches of rein at a time.
  3. Encourage the horse to follow the contact downward by softening your elbows and arms forward.
  4. The horse should stretch its neck forward and down, seeking the contact as it goes lower.
  5. Maintain the walk rhythm. The stretch shouldn't cause the horse to shuffle or slow down.
  6. After 1-2 minutes of stretching, gradually take up the reins to return to a working frame.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Dropping the reins entirely instead of gradually lengthening them.
  • The horse lifting its head when the contact is softened, indicating it wasn't truly through.
  • Losing the walk rhythm during the stretch.
  • Rounding the rider's back and collapsing the core when reaching forward with the hands.

Variations & Progressions

  • Perform the stretch at the trot on a 20-meter circle.
  • Add changes of direction while maintaining the stretched frame.
  • Use the stretchy walk as your standard cool-down for every ride.
Tip: The stretch reveals your training quality. A horse that stretches forward-down is using its topline correctly and is relaxed.
