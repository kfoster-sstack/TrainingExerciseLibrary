Introduction to Neck Reining at the Collected Trot

Focus: Steering, Communication, Responsiveness

Equipment: Western saddle pad, 4 cones or markers

Arena Diagram

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. At the collected collected trot, hold both reins in your left hand (standard western rein hold).
  2. To turn right, lay the left rein across the horse's neck while looking right and shifting your weight slightly right.
  3. Your right leg stays at the girth. Your left leg presses slightly behind the girth to push the haunches through the turn.
  4. The horse should turn from the neck rein pressure, not from direct rein pulling.
  5. Practice turns around the arena using neck rein cues. Set up cones to weave through.
  6. If the horse doesn't respond to the neck rein alone, add a brief direct rein cue with the right hand, then return to neck rein.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Pulling the direct rein instead of laying the indirect rein across the neck.
  • Not using leg aids to support the turn — neck reining alone isn't enough.
  • Leaning dramatically into the turn instead of keeping your weight centered.
  • Moving both hands instead of keeping the rein hand still and guiding with the indirect rein.

Variations & Progressions

  • Practice neck reining at the jog for increased challenge.
  • Weave through a line of 6 cones using only neck rein cues.
  • Use neck reining to open a gate from horseback.
Tip: Neck reining is a gradual education. Start with direct rein + neck rein together, then fade out the direct rein over weeks.
