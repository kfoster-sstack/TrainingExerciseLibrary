Flatwork / Dressage Intermediate 20 min

Volte at the Trot

Focus: Collection, Bend, Engagement

Equipment: 2 cones or markers

Arena Diagram

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Set up two cones 8 meters apart. You will ride a volte (small circle) using these as diameter points.
  2. Pick up a sitting trot. The sitting trot gives you better control of the horse's body on a tight circle.
  3. Begin the volte, keeping your inside leg firmly at the girth to create and maintain bend. Your outside leg guards the haunches.
  4. Half-halt with the outside rein every quarter of the circle to prevent the horse from falling onto the inside shoulder.
  5. Ride 3 voltes, then straighten out for 10-12 strides on the long side. This gives the horse a mental and physical break.
  6. Return to the volte. On this second set, focus on keeping the hind legs stepping actively under the body.
  7. Change direction and repeat. Keep sessions short since volte work is physically demanding.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • The horse falling onto the inside shoulder and leaning through the turn.
  • Sitting too heavily on one seat bone, throwing the horse off balance.
  • The volte becoming egg-shaped because the horse drifts at one point.
  • Keeping the horse on the volte too long, causing fatigue and resistance.

Variations & Progressions

  • Ride the volte at the canter (advanced) — this requires significant collection.
  • Chain voltes down the long side: volte at E, straight 5 strides, volte at B.
  • Exit the volte into shoulder-in for a seamless lateral movement transition.
Tip: If your horse braces against the bend, ride one large circle first to establish the bend, then gradually decrease the size.
