Jumping Intermediate 30 min

Bounce to Bending Line at the Sitting Trot

Focus: Technique, Agility, Track Planning

Equipment: 6 jump poles, 6 jump standards

Arena Diagram

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Set up a bounce (2 fences 10 feet apart), then a single fence on a bending line 60 feet from the bounce exit.
  2. Sitting Trot into the bounce. The bounce develops quick reflexes and front leg technique.
  3. After the bounce, sitting trot on a bending line to the single fence in 4-5 strides.
  4. The bending line requires you to establish a track and maintain rhythm immediately after the demanding bounce.
  5. Practice the bending line to both left and right turns.
  6. This combination tests both gymnastic ability and course-riding skill in one exercise.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Losing balance in the bounce and not recovering in time for the bending line.
  • Not planning the bending line turn early enough — decide your direction before the bounce.
  • The canter after the bounce being too fast or unbalanced for the bending line.
  • Cutting the bending line too tight and arriving at the third fence too close.

Variations & Progressions

  • Change the bending line distance to 48 feet (3 strides direct, 4 strides bending).
  • Add a fourth fence after the bending line for a mini-course.
  • Replace the bounce with a one-stride gymnastic for a different entry.
Tip: This exercise bridges the gap between gymnastic training and course riding. It teaches you to think quickly after a demanding effort.
