Groundwork Intermediate 20 min

Free Lunging in a Round Pen

Focus: Communication, Respect, Liberty

Equipment: Round pen (50-60 ft diameter)

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Enter the round pen with your horse at liberty (no lunge line). Stand in the center.
  2. Use your body position to drive the horse forward: step toward the haunches and raise your energy.
  3. To change direction, step in front of the horse's driveline (shoulder area) and redirect it.
  4. Practice walk, trot, and halt transitions using only body language and voice.
  5. The horse should maintain the gait you request and change when you ask.
  6. After 10-15 minutes, ask the horse to face you and allow you to approach. This is the 'join up.'

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Chasing the horse with high energy instead of using controlled, directional body language.
  • Standing in one spot and not using the angles of the round pen to direct the horse.
  • Getting frustrated when the horse doesn't respond and escalating to aggressive behavior.
  • Not recognizing the signs of the horse wanting to 'join up' (licking, chewing, inside ear on you, lowering head).

Variations & Progressions

  • Practice specific patterns: circle, change direction, circle, halt, back up.
  • Introduce liberty obstacles in the round pen for variety.
  • Work toward calling the horse to you from across the round pen.
Tip: Free lunging reveals the truth of your relationship. A horse that turns in to face you wants to be with you. That's the ultimate training goal.
