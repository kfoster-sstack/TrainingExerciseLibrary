Flatwork / Dressage Beginner 15 min

Progressive Transitions

Focus: Transitions, Rhythm, Obedience

Equipment: Letter markers

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Start at the halt on the center line. Prepare mentally for a series of upward transitions.
  2. Ask for walk. Walk for 8-10 strides with energy and rhythm.
  3. Ask for trot. Trot for 8-10 strides with a steady tempo.
  4. Ask for canter. Canter for 8-10 strides on the correct lead.
  5. Now reverse: canter to trot (8-10 strides), trot to walk (8-10 strides), walk to halt.
  6. The entire sequence should feel like a smooth escalator — each gait builds on the last.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Rushing the upward transitions so they feel abrupt and unbalanced.
  • The horse breaking to trot during the canter phase because of insecure aids.
  • Downward transitions that take too long (dribbling through 15 strides instead of 8-10).
  • Losing rhythm or straightness during any of the transitions.

Variations & Progressions

  • Reduce to 5 strides per gait for a more advanced version.
  • Add halt-trot and halt-canter transitions to skip gaits.
  • Perform the progressive transitions on a 20-meter circle instead of a straight line.
Tip: This exercise is like playing musical scales. The goal is evenness and flow between each gait.
