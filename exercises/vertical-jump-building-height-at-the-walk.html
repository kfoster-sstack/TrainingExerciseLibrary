Jumping Beginner 20 min

Vertical Jump: Building Height at the Walk

Focus: Technique, Confidence, Scope

Equipment: 2 jump poles, 2 jump standards

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Start with a vertical at 2 feet. Approach at the working walk.
  2. Maintain rhythm and impulsion. The horse needs to use its hind legs to clear a vertical.
  3. After 3 successful jumps at 2 feet, raise to 2'3". Repeat 3 clean jumps.
  4. Continue raising in 3-inch increments: 2'6", 2'9", 3'0" (if your level permits).
  5. At each height, evaluate: is the horse jumping cleanly? Is it rocking back on the haunches for takeoff?
  6. Stop at the first sign of struggling or loss of form. Quality over height.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Raising the fence before the horse is consistently clean at the current height.
  • Getting greedy with height and pushing past the horse's current ability.
  • The horse hanging its front legs because it's reaching instead of jumping with technique.
  • The rider getting behind the motion as the fences get bigger.

Variations & Progressions

  • Add a placing pole to maintain consistent takeoff distances.
  • Compare the horse's technique over a vertical vs. an oxer at the same height.
  • Use a gymnastic approach (trot in to one-stride) for height building.
Tip: Height comes from technique, not courage. A horse that uses its body correctly at 2 feet will be safe at 3 feet.
