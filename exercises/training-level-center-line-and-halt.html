Flatwork / Dressage Beginner 15 min

Training Level Center Line and Halt

Focus: Accuracy, Straightness, Transitions

Equipment: Letter markers

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Turn down the center line at A at the working trot.
  2. Focus your eyes on C. Ride a perfectly straight line.
  3. At X, prepare to halt. Half-halt 3 strides before X.
  4. Halt at X. The halt should be square with the horse standing straight on the center line.
  5. Stand immobile for 3 seconds. Then proceed at the working trot toward C.
  6. At C, turn left or right onto the track. The turn should be smooth and balanced.
  7. Repeat the center line entry and halt 4-6 times, alternating the direction at C.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Drifting off the center line because the horse is drawn to the rail or other horses.
  • The halt not being square — one leg trailing or the horse standing crooked.
  • Moving at the halt (fidgeting, stepping, swishing tail).
  • The transition from halt to trot being sluggish or explosive.

Variations & Progressions

  • Add a rein-back after the halt for Training Level Test 3 preparation.
  • Practice the entry at medium trot for Second Level test preparation.
  • Film the entry from C to assess your straightness objectively.
Tip: First impressions matter in dressage tests. Your center line entry is the judge's first view of your horse. Make it count.
