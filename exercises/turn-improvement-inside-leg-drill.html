Turn Improvement: Inside Leg Drill

Focus: Turns, Technique, Responsiveness

Equipment: 1 barrel

Arena Diagram

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. This drill focuses on the inside leg — the most important aid in barrel turns.
  2. Set up a single barrel. Approach at the jog.
  3. As you enter the turn, press your inside leg firmly at the girth.
  4. Your inside leg pushes the horse's ribcage outward, preventing the horse from dropping its shoulder into the barrel.
  5. The inside leg also maintains impulsion through the turn so the horse doesn't stall.
  6. Practice 8-10 turns, focusing solely on the inside leg feeling.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Pulling the horse around the barrel with the inside rein instead of pushing with the inside leg.
  • Collapsing the inside hip, which drops your weight into the turn.
  • Not enough inside leg, causing the horse to fall in toward the barrel.
  • Focusing on speed instead of the inside leg technique.

Variations & Progressions

  • Practice the inside leg aid on circles of different sizes.
  • Close your eyes for 2 strides of the turn to focus on feel.
  • Compare turns with strong inside leg vs. turns with primarily rein aids.
Tip: Your inside leg is your barrel-saving tool. A strong inside leg keeps the horse's shoulder up and body arced, preventing knocked barrels.
