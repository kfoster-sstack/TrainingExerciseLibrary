Flatwork / Dressage Intermediate 15 min

Trot-Halt-Trot on the Center Line

Focus: Transitions, Precision, Balance

Equipment: Letter markers

Arena Diagram

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Enter the center line at A at the working trot.
  2. Halt at D (the first quarter-line marker area). Stand for 2-3 seconds.
  3. Proceed at the trot to X. Halt again. Stand for 2-3 seconds.
  4. Proceed at the trot to G. Halt a final time. Stand for 2-3 seconds.
  5. Proceed at the trot to C. Turn onto the track.
  6. Each halt must be square and on the center line. Each trot departure must be prompt and balanced.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Drifting off the center line between halts.
  • The halts not being square because the horse anticipates moving again.
  • Over-preparing the halt so the horse stops early.
  • The trot departures being sluggish from repeated halts.

Variations & Progressions

  • Add rein-back between each halt for a more advanced version.
  • Perform at the canter with halt transitions.
  • Use the exercise as a warm-up for test preparation.
Tip: Multiple halts on the center line test your horse's patience and your precision. Each halt should feel like a deliberate choice, not an accident.
