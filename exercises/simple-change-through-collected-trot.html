Flatwork / Dressage Intermediate 30 min

Simple Change Through Collected Trot

Focus: Transitions, Collection, Balance

Equipment: Letter markers

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Begin at the working collected trot, tracking right on a 20-meter circle.
  2. At X (or a designated point), transition from collected trot to collected collected trot. Allow 3-5 clear collected collected trot steps.
  3. After the collected collected trot steps, pick up the left lead collected trot — the new inside lead.
  4. Ride the 20-meter circle on the new lead for one full revolution.
  5. At X again, collected collected trot for 3-5 steps and pick up the right lead.
  6. Repeat the simple change 4-6 times. Gradually reduce the number of collected collected trot steps to 3.
  7. The collected collected trot steps should be clear, forward, and balanced — not shuffling or rushing.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • The horse trotting instead of walking between canter leads.
  • Too many walk strides, losing the connection between the two canters.
  • Picking up the wrong lead on the second canter depart.
  • The horse becoming tense and anticipating the lead change.

Variations & Progressions

  • Perform simple changes on a figure-eight, changing lead at X.
  • Reduce to 2-3 walk strides for a more advanced simple change.
  • Use simple changes as preparation for flying changes by progressive reduction of walk strides.
Tip: Count the walk steps out loud: 'one, two, three, canter.' This keeps you disciplined about the number of steps.
