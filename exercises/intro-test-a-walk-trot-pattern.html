Flatwork / Dressage Beginner 20 min

Intro Test A Walk-Trot Pattern

Focus: Accuracy, Transitions, Precision

Equipment: Letter markers

Arena Diagram

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. This simulates a basic Intro Level dressage test pattern. Enter at A at the working trot.
  2. Halt at X. Salute (nod). Proceed at the working trot.
  3. Track right at C. Ride the long side, then across the diagonal H-X-F.
  4. At F, continue tracking left. Ride a 20-meter circle at A.
  5. Continue on the track. Free walk across the next diagonal, then pick up trot before the corner.
  6. Ride the remaining pattern, finishing with a halt at X. Salute.
  7. Practice the complete pattern 2-3 times, focusing on accuracy and smooth transitions.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Not halting at X precisely — stopping too early or too late on the center line.
  • The diagonal line drifting instead of being straight from letter to letter.
  • Forgetting the pattern mid-test and losing your place.
  • Not preparing transitions early enough, causing them to happen at the wrong spot.

Variations & Progressions

  • Practice individual movements from the test in isolation before stringing them together.
  • Have someone call the test for you while you ride.
  • Ride the test at the walk first to memorize the pattern without worrying about trot balance.
Tip: Memorize the test by drawing it on paper, then walking it on foot in the arena. Ride it only after you can walk it perfectly.
