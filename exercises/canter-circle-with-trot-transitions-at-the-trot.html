Canter Circle with Trot Transitions at the Trot

Focus: Transitions, Balance, Engagement

Equipment: 4 cones or markers

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Set up a 20-meter circle. Begin at the working trot on the correct lead.
  2. At the first quarter-point cone, transition to trot for 4-6 strides.
  3. At the next quarter-point, pick up the trot again. The transition should be prompt and on the correct lead.
  4. Continue this pattern: trot three-quarters, trot one-quarter, around the circle.
  5. After 3 repetitions, reverse the ratio: trot three-quarters and trot only one-quarter of the circle.
  6. The trot-to-trot transitions on a circle test your horse's responsiveness and balance.
  7. Finish with a full circle of balanced trot, then trot and stretch.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Picking up the wrong lead after the trot interlude.
  • The trot section becoming rushed as the horse anticipates the canter depart.
  • Losing the circle shape during transitions.
  • The canter becoming flat and running after multiple transitions.

Variations & Progressions

  • Use walk transitions instead of trot for a more advanced collection exercise.
  • Reduce to 2-3 trot strides between canter segments for sharper transitions.
  • Perform on a 15-meter circle to increase the balance demand.
Tip: If the horse picks up the wrong lead, calmly trot and try again. Don't punish, just redirect.
