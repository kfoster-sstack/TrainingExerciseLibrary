Flatwork / Dressage Intermediate 20 min

Yielding the Inside Rein on a Circle at the Canter

Focus: Balance, Self-Carriage, Suppleness

Equipment: 4 cones or markers

Arena Diagram

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Establish a balanced working canter on a 20-meter circle with correct bend.
  2. When the circle feels steady and balanced, briefly give away the inside rein by pushing your hand forward 4-6 inches.
  3. The horse should maintain the circle, the bend, and the tempo without the inside rein for 3-5 strides.
  4. Take back the inside rein and re-establish your normal contact.
  5. If the horse falls in or loses bend when you release the inside rein, the horse is leaning on that rein rather than carrying itself.
  6. Repeat the give-and-take 4-5 times per direction. The horse should improve with each attempt.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Giving the rein too suddenly, startling the horse and causing a wobble.
  • The horse immediately falling to the inside, proving it was leaning on the inside rein.
  • Not giving enough rein — a tiny 1-inch give doesn't test anything.
  • Only testing on the horse's easy side and avoiding the difficult side.

Variations & Progressions

  • Try yielding both reins briefly for a true self-carriage test.
  • Perform the test at the canter for increased difficulty.
  • Give the inside rein on a 15-meter circle where balance is harder.
Tip: This test tells you the truth: if your horse can't hold the circle without the inside rein, it's not truly balanced and through.
