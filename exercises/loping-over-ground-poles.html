Western Beginner 20 min

Loping Over Ground Poles

Focus: Rhythm, Awareness, Stride Control

Equipment: 4 ground poles

Arena Diagram

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Set up 4 ground poles spaced 6 feet apart (comfortable lope stride).
  2. Approach at the lope. The horse should lope through the poles with one stride between each.
  3. Maintain a steady lope rhythm. Look ahead past the poles, not down at them.
  4. The horse should pick up its feet deliberately, not lazily drag over the poles.
  5. Practice from both leads to develop equal coordination.
  6. This exercise improves the horse's body awareness and the rider's feel for stride length.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Breaking to the jog before or during the poles.
  • The horse tripping over poles because it's not paying attention.
  • Looking down at the poles, which drops the horse onto the forehand.
  • The lope becoming rushed or choppy through the poles.

Variations & Progressions

  • Raise alternate poles for more engagement.
  • Increase spacing to 7 feet for a longer stride.
  • Add a single small jump after the poles for a transition exercise.
Tip: Ground poles at the lope develop the cadence and foot awareness needed for trail patterns and ranch riding classes.
