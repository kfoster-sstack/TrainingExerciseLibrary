Western Intermediate 25 min

Rollback from the Fence

Focus: Agility, Responsiveness, Collection

Equipment: None required

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Lope toward the arena fence on a straight line.
  2. At the fence, halt using your seat and voice. The horse should stop facing the fence.
  3. Immediately ask for a 180-degree turn (pivot on the haunches) away from the fence.
  4. Depart at the lope in the new direction on the new lead.
  5. The entire sequence — stop, turn, depart — should be smooth and connected.
  6. Practice rollbacks to both directions, alternating each time.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • The horse stopping too far from the fence or not stopping at all.
  • The 180-degree turn being too wide, losing the tight pivot.
  • Not departing immediately after the turn — there should be no hesitation.
  • Picking up the wrong lead on the departure.

Variations & Progressions

  • Add speed to the approach for a more dramatic stop-and-rollback.
  • Chain rollbacks: stop, roll left, lope, stop, roll right, lope.
  • Use rollbacks as part of a full reining pattern practice.
Tip: The rollback is a stop, a pivot, and a departure fused into one movement. Practice each piece separately before combining.
