Speed / Barrel Racing Intermediate 25 min

Turn and Drive: Exit Speed at the Canter

Focus: Acceleration, Power, Turn Quality

Equipment: 1 barrel, 2 cones or markers

Arena Diagram

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Set up a barrel with a cone 30 feet away marking where the horse should be at full speed after the turn.
  2. Approach the barrel at the canter. Rate, turn, and then immediately drive out of the turn toward the cone.
  3. The acceleration out of the turn should be explosive. Use your legs, voice, and body to push the horse forward.
  4. The horse should reach full speed by the time it passes the cone.
  5. The turn-to-acceleration transition is where races are won. No hesitation after the turn.
  6. Practice 6-8 times, then switch to the other direction.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • The horse stalling after the turn instead of driving forward immediately.
  • The rider not supporting the acceleration with strong leg and body aids.
  • The turn being so tight that the horse loses momentum and can't accelerate.
  • Not looking ahead toward the next barrel or the finish line during the drive-out.

Variations & Progressions

  • Increase the distance to the cone for a longer acceleration zone.
  • Chain two barrels: turn barrel 1, drive to barrel 2, turn barrel 2.
  • Time the distance from the barrel to the cone to measure exit speed improvement.
Tip: The fastest barrel racers aren't the fastest runners — they're the quickest out of the turn. Turn quality plus immediate acceleration equals winning times.
