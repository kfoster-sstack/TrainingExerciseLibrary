Flatwork / Dressage Intermediate 20 min

Working to Collected Trot Transitions

Focus: Collection, Engagement, Balance

Equipment: Letter markers

Arena Diagram

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. At the working trot, ride a 20-meter circle. Establish a steady, rhythmic tempo.
  2. At a designated point, half-halt firmly and ask the horse to shorten the stride without losing energy.
  3. The collected trot should feel like the horse is sitting more on the haunches, with shorter but higher steps.
  4. Maintain collected trot for half the circle, then allow the horse back to working trot.
  5. The transition between working and collected should be gradual — think of a dimmer switch, not an on-off switch.
  6. Repeat the transitions 4-5 times, then change direction.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • The horse simply slowing down instead of truly collecting (shorter, higher steps).
  • Losing impulsion in the collection — the horse becomes lazy instead of more engaged.
  • Over-collecting so the horse becomes tight and tense in the back.
  • Not allowing enough return to working trot between collection phases.

Variations & Progressions

  • Progress to extended trot from collected trot for the full range of adjustability.
  • Perform the transitions on the long side at specific letters.
  • Add collected trot to walk transitions for further collection work.
Tip: Collection is not slow. It is the same energy in a shorter stride. Think of compressing a spring — same force, smaller space.
