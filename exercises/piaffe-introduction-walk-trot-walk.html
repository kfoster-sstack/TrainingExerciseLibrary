Piaffe Introduction: Walk-Trot-Walk

Focus: Collection, Engagement, Power

Equipment: Dressage whip

Arena Diagram

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Piaffe is the ultimate collected trot: the horse trots in place. This introduction builds the concept gradually.
  2. At the collected walk, use both legs rhythmically to ask the horse for more energy without more forward speed.
  3. Touch the horse behind your leg with a dressage whip in a rhythmic tap to encourage more activity.
  4. The horse should begin taking shorter, higher steps — somewhere between walk and trot.
  5. Don't ask for a full piaffe yet. Accept any increase in energy and step height as progress.
  6. Release the aids and walk forward on a long rein. Repeat 3-4 times maximum per session.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Asking for too much too soon — piaffe takes months or years to develop.
  • The horse running forward instead of collecting the energy upward.
  • Over-use of the whip creating tension and anxiety instead of engagement.
  • Not rewarding small improvements, frustrating the horse.

Variations & Progressions

  • Work with a trainer on the ground who can assist with the whip timing.
  • Practice in-hand piaffe before attempting under saddle.
  • Combine with transitions: collected walk to piaffe steps to collected trot forward.
Tip: Piaffe is not something you force. It emerges from progressive collection work over many months. Celebrate each small step of progress.
