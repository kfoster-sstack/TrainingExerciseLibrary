Spin Introduction at the Canter

Focus: Precision, Responsiveness, Agility

Equipment: 4 cones or markers

Arena Diagram

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. At the halt, ask the horse for a turn on the haunches to the right: left neck rein, right leg at the girth.
  2. The horse should pivot on the right hind leg while the front feet cross over in a 360-degree turn.
  3. Start with quarter-turns (90 degrees). Halt and reward.
  4. Build to half-turns (180 degrees), then full turns (360 degrees).
  5. The pivot foot (inside hind) should stay planted in one spot. The outside hind leg steps around it.
  6. Keep the turn at a canter tempo. Speed comes later, only after accuracy.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • The pivot foot walking a large circle instead of staying in one spot.
  • The horse walking forward through the turn instead of pivoting.
  • Using too much rein and not enough leg for the turn.
  • Trying to spin fast before the horse understands the basic pivot.

Variations & Progressions

  • Progress to spinning at the jog tempo for speed.
  • Build to multiple revolutions: 2 spins, 3 spins, 4 spins.
  • Practice spins in both directions for equal development.
Tip: A spin is just a fast turn on the haunches. If the horse can't do a slow, accurate pivot, don't add speed.
