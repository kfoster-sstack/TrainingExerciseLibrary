Tarp Desensitization at the Collected Trot

Focus: Confidence, Desensitization, Trust

Equipment: Small tarp or plastic sheet, Halter and leadrope

Arena Diagram

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Start with the tarp folded up and on the ground. Lead your horse toward it at the collected collected trot.
  2. Allow the horse to see, sniff, and investigate the tarp. Don't rush this step.
  3. If the horse approaches calmly, unfold the tarp slightly and repeat the approach.
  4. Gradually unfold the tarp until it's fully open. Lead the horse around it, then over a corner.
  5. If the horse collected collected trots over any part of the tarp, praise lavishly. The crinkling sound is often the scariest part.
  6. Work toward the horse collected collected troting across the entire tarp calmly.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Forcing the horse onto the tarp before it's comfortable with the sight and sound.
  • Punishing fear reactions — spooking is instinct, not disobedience.
  • Moving too fast through the stages. If the horse is worried at step 2, don't jump to step 5.
  • Not rewarding brave behavior (even small steps toward the tarp).

Variations & Progressions

  • Drape the tarp over the horse's back while it stands still.
  • Ride the horse over the tarp once it's comfortable being led.
  • Use different textures: plastic bags, bubble wrap, foam mats.
Tip: Fear-based training creates a fearful horse. Patience-based training creates a confident horse. Let the horse set the pace.
