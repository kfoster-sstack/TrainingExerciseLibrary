Long-Lining Circles at the Canter

Focus: Steering, Bend, Collection

Equipment: Long lines, Surcingle or saddle

Arena Diagram

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. From the cantering straight line, ask the horse to turn onto a large circle (20 meters).
  2. The inside line creates the bend, the outside line supports and controls the speed.
  3. Canter the circle with the horse. You will be following behind on a smaller inner circle.
  4. Maintain the horse on the circle for 2-3 revolutions, then straighten and go the other way.
  5. The challenge is keeping the lines from tangling while managing bend and forward motion.
  6. Practice until the horse bends smoothly on the circle with light, even contact on both lines.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • The outside line going slack and losing contact, allowing the horse to fall out.
  • The handler getting too close to the horse's hindquarters on the turn.
  • Lines crossing over the horse's back or getting under the tail.
  • The horse overbending the neck because the inside line has too much pressure.

Variations & Progressions

  • Reduce circle size to 15 meters for more collection demand.
  • Add trot on the circle for a more challenging exercise.
  • Practice figure-eights with smooth direction changes through the center.
Tip: Keep the lines at hip height on the horse. If they drop below the hocks, you risk tangling.
