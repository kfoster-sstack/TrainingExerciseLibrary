Lead Change After a Fence

Focus: Transitions, Balance, Lead Control

Equipment: 2 jump poles, 2 jump standards

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Jump a small fence on the center line.
  2. After landing, immediately plan a turn to the left (requiring left lead) or right (requiring right lead).
  3. If you need the left lead after the fence, look left and apply your right leg behind the girth on landing.
  4. If you get the wrong lead, trot for 3-4 strides and pick up the correct lead before the turn.
  5. Practice getting specific leads after the fence by planning your turn before you jump.
  6. This is essential for course riding where you need specific leads for the next line.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Not planning the lead before the fence, then scrambling after landing.
  • Landing so crooked that the lead change is impossible.
  • Forcing the lead change with a dramatic body shift that unbalances the horse.
  • Always turning the same direction and never practicing the other lead.

Variations & Progressions

  • Set up two fences requiring different leads: jump fence 1, get left lead, turn to fence 2.
  • Practice getting both leads from the same fence by alternating turn directions.
  • Add a tight rollback turn after landing to practice lead and turn combination.
Tip: Your eye directs the lead. Look where you're going before you leave the ground, and your body will naturally set up the correct lead.
