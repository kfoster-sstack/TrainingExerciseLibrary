Flatwork / Dressage Intermediate 20 min

Canter-Trot Transitions on the Long Side

Focus: Transitions, Responsiveness, Rhythm

Equipment: Letter markers

Arena Diagram

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Track right at the working canter along the long side.
  2. At the first letter (H, S, E, V, or K), ask for an upward transition to working canter.
  3. Close your lower legs and allow your hips to follow the new rhythm. Keep your hands steady.
  4. Canter to the next letter, then ask for a downward transition back to canter.
  5. The downward transition should come from your seat and core: stop following the canter rhythm and close your fingers on the reins.
  6. Alternate canter and canter at every letter along the long side.
  7. Focus on making each transition happen exactly at the letter — not 3 strides early or late.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • The transition happening too late because the rider doesn't prepare early enough.
  • The trot departure being abrupt and fast instead of flowing forward smoothly.
  • The walk transition taking too many strides — the horse leaking forward instead of responding promptly.
  • The rider using too much rein for the downward transition instead of the seat and core.

Variations & Progressions

  • Add halt transitions between the walk-trot transitions for three-gait work.
  • Progress to trot-canter transitions at the letters.
  • Perform the transitions on a 20-meter circle instead of the long side.
Tip: Prepare your transition 3 strides before the letter. Think of it as: prepare, ask, allow.
