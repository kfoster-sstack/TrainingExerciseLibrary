Flatwork / Dressage Beginner 15 min

Half-10-Meter Circle to Change Direction

Focus: Bend, Accuracy, Transitions

Equipment: 2 cones or markers

Arena Diagram

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. At the working trot, tracking right, begin a half-circle of 10 meters off the track at E or B.
  2. The half-circle brings you to the center line, facing the opposite direction.
  3. Ride 3-4 strides on the center line, perfectly straight, to establish the new direction.
  4. Then ride a half-circle back to the track on the new rein (now tracking left).
  5. The two half-circles create a smooth S-shaped direction change through the center of the arena.
  6. Practice this movement 4-6 times, alternating the starting side.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • The half-circles not being symmetrical — one larger than the other.
  • Not straightening on the center line between the two half-circles.
  • The horse falling in on the second half-circle because the new bend isn't established.
  • Losing rhythm during the direction change.

Variations & Progressions

  • Use 8-meter half-circles for a more collected version.
  • Perform the direction change at the canter with a simple change on the center line.
  • Add a transition on the center line: trot-walk-trot between the half-circles.
Tip: This is a standard dressage test movement. The quality depends on equal-sized half-circles and a straight center line section.
