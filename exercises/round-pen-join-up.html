Groundwork Intermediate 25 min

Round Pen Join-Up

Focus: Communication, Bond, Trust

Equipment: Round pen (50-60 ft diameter)

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Enter the round pen with your horse at liberty. Stand in the center and establish your presence.
  2. Drive the horse forward on the circle by stepping toward its haunches with assertive body language.
  3. Allow the horse to trot or canter around you. Watch for signs of attention: inside ear turning toward you.
  4. When the horse shows submission signs (licking, chewing, lowering head, inside ear), soften your body language.
  5. Turn your body sideways to the horse, lower your energy, and look at the ground.
  6. The horse should turn and walk toward you. Stand still and let it approach. Rub its forehead when it arrives.
  7. Walk away. If the horse follows, the join-up is successful.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Being too aggressive with body language, causing the horse to panic instead of think.
  • Not reading the horse's body language and missing the submission signals.
  • Chasing the horse aggressively instead of using directional pressure.
  • Not softening when the horse shows willingness to connect.

Variations & Progressions

  • Practice join-up with different horses to refine your body language skills.
  • After join-up, add leading at liberty for a comprehensive bonding session.
  • Use join-up as the first exercise with a new or untrusted horse.
Tip: Join-up is communication, not domination. The horse chooses to be with you because your body language invites it. Force defeats the purpose.
