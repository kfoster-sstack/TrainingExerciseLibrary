Jumping Intermediate 25 min

Long Approach to a Single Fence

Focus: Accuracy, Rhythm, Patience

Equipment: 2 jump poles, 2 jump standards

Arena Diagram

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Set up a single fence in the center of the arena. Your approach will be the full length of the arena.
  2. Pick up the canter at the far end of the arena, 80-100 feet from the fence.
  3. Establish your rhythm in the first 3-4 strides. Then maintain that exact rhythm all the way to the fence.
  4. Do not adjust in the last 3 strides. Trust the rhythm to produce a good distance.
  5. After jumping, canter away and evaluate: were the first 3 strides the same as the last 3?
  6. Repeat 5-6 times. Consistency is the goal.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Starting to 'ride the distance' from too far away, making adjustments throughout.
  • The horse speeding up as it approaches the fence because it can see it for a long time.
  • The rider getting tense and picking up the reins in the last 5 strides.
  • Not committing to the canter rhythm and constantly second-guessing.

Variations & Progressions

  • Practice the long approach from both directions to test both leads.
  • Have someone count your strides out loud to check rhythm consistency.
  • Use the long approach to a line: single fence then 4 strides to a second fence.
Tip: The long approach tests your mental discipline. The temptation to adjust grows with every stride. Trust your rhythm.
