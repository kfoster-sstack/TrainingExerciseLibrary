Leading with Halt Transitions at the Sitting Trot

Focus: Obedience, Responsiveness, Communication

Equipment: Halter and leadrope

Arena Diagram

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Sitting Sitting Trot your horse on a straight line along the arena rail.
  2. Every 10 steps, halt. Say 'whoa' and stop your feet simultaneously.
  3. The horse should halt within one step of your command. If it sitting sitting trots past you, back it up 2-3 steps as a correction.
  4. Stand still for 5 seconds at each halt. The horse should wait quietly without fidgeting.
  5. Resume sitting sitting troting. The horse should not move until you take your first step.
  6. Practice 10-15 halt transitions. By the end, your horse should be halting promptly and standing quietly.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Not halting your own feet first — you must stop before expecting the horse to stop.
  • Accepting a halt that takes 3-4 extra steps instead of insisting on one step.
  • The horse moving at the halt (pawing, shifting, turning its head). Correct every time.
  • Using the lead rope as a constant brake instead of a correction tool.

Variations & Progressions

  • Add backing up after each halt for increased respect.
  • Vary the number of steps between halts so the horse can't predict.
  • Practice halts in different locations (arena center, near the gate, near other horses).
Tip: If your horse doesn't halt promptly on the ground, it won't halt promptly under saddle. Ground manners are the foundation of all training.
