Groundwork Beginner 10 min

Carrot Stretches: Chin to Chest at the Trot

Focus: Flexibility, Topline, Recovery

Equipment: Horse treats

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Stand in front of your horse with a treat.
  2. Offer the treat low, between the horse's front legs, encouraging it to tuck its chin toward its chest.
  3. The horse should round its neck and back, stretching the topline muscles.
  4. Hold for 5-10 seconds when the horse reaches the treat.
  5. Repeat 3-5 times. This stretches the muscles along the top of the neck and back.
  6. This is the opposite of the lateral stretch — it stretches the longitudinal muscles.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • The horse stepping forward to reach the treat instead of stretching down.
  • Not holding the stretch long enough to be beneficial.
  • Rushing through the exercise without allowing the horse to relax into the stretch.
  • The horse only going halfway down because it's stiff — this improves with daily practice.

Variations & Progressions

  • Progress to reaching further back between the front legs for a deeper stretch.
  • Combine with lateral stretches for a complete stretching routine.
  • Add this to your daily grooming routine for consistency.
Tip: The chin-to-chest stretch is the most important carrot stretch. It directly targets the muscles your horse needs for a round topline.
