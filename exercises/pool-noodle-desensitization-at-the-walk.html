Pool Noodle Desensitization at the Walk

Focus: Confidence, Desensitization, Trust

Equipment: 2 pool noodles, Halter and leadrope

Arena Diagram

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Hold a pool noodle in one hand while leading your horse. Let the horse see and smell it.
  2. Gently touch the horse's shoulder with the noodle. If it stands still, praise and remove the noodle.
  3. Gradually work the noodle along the neck, back, and hindquarters. Go slowly and reward calm behavior.
  4. Progress to rubbing the noodle over the horse's legs (this is usually scarier).
  5. Wave the noodle gently in the air near the horse. The movement is more challenging than the touch.
  6. The goal: the horse stands quietly while you wave pool noodles all around its body.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Starting with the noodle on a sensitive area (legs, belly) instead of the shoulder.
  • Moving the noodle too quickly, startling the horse.
  • Not retreating when the horse shows signs of stress (high head, tight muscles, wide eyes).
  • Only desensitizing on one side — horses need both-side exposure.

Variations & Progressions

  • Hang pool noodles from a line for the horse to walk through like a curtain.
  • Use the noodle while the horse is tied for standing-still practice.
  • Progress to plastic bags on a stick for a noisier challenge.
Tip: Pool noodles are the best desensitization tool because they're soft, colorful, and make interesting sounds without causing harm.
