Jumping Intermediate 30 min

Combination: In-and-Out

Focus: Technique, Balance, Responsiveness

Equipment: 4 jump poles, 4 jump standards

Arena Diagram

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Set up two small fences 24 feet apart (one stride). This is an in-and-out combination.
  2. Approach at the working canter. After the first fence, the horse has exactly one stride before the second.
  3. Keep your leg on between the fences. The horse should jump in, stride, and jump out smoothly.
  4. Your upper body should stay centered — don't lean forward between the fences.
  5. Practice from both directions (the distances may feel different depending on the horse).
  6. This is a standard combination found in almost every jumping course.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • The horse adding a stride (getting 2 strides instead of 1) due to lack of impulsion.
  • The rider getting ahead of the motion on the first fence and interfering with the stride.
  • Not enough leg between the fences, causing the horse to stop at the second element.
  • The horse jumping flat, not using its body correctly in the short distance.

Variations & Progressions

  • Change to a bounce (10 feet, no stride) for more technical demand.
  • Add a third element for a one-to-one-stride combination.
  • Raise the second element higher than the first for scope development.
Tip: Combinations test your horse's training more than any other element. If the flatwork is correct, the combination rides itself.
